📚 Mastering English Tenses

Tenses are the backbone of English grammar. They tell us when an action happens – in the present, past, or future. Understanding tenses is essential for BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs, HSC, and university admission exams. (Tenses ইংরেজি ব্যাকরণের মেরুদণ্ড। তারা বলে যে একটি কাজ কখন ঘটে – বর্তমানে, অতীতে বা ভবিষ্যতে।)

📌 The 12 English Tenses

Tense Example Bangla Usage
Present Indefinite I eat rice. আমি ভাত খাই। Habitual actions, facts
Present Continuous I am eating rice. আমি ভাত খাচ্ছি। Action happening now
Present Perfect I have eaten rice. আমি ভাত খেয়েছি। Past action with present result
Present Perfect Continuous I have been eating rice for 10 minutes. আমি ১০ মিনিট ধরে ভাত খাচ্ছি। Action started in past and continues
Past Indefinite I ate rice. আমি ভাত খেয়েছিলাম। Completed action in the past
Past Continuous I was eating rice. আমি ভাত খাচ্ছিলাম। Ongoing action in the past
Past Perfect I had eaten rice. আমি ভাত খেয়েছিলাম (তার আগে)। Action completed before another past action
Past Perfect Continuous I had been eating rice for 10 minutes. আমি ১০ মিনিট ধরে ভাত খাচ্ছিলাম। Ongoing action up to a point in the past
Future Indefinite I will eat rice. আমি ভাত খাব। Action in the future
Future Continuous I will be eating rice. আমি ভাত খাচ্ছি থাকব। Ongoing action in the future
Future Perfect I will have eaten rice. আমি ভাত খেয়ে ফেলব। Action completed by a specific future time
Future Perfect Continuous I will have been eating rice for 10 minutes. আমি ১০ মিনিট ধরে ভাত খাচ্ছি থাকব। Ongoing action up to a future time

❌ Common Tense Mistakes

❌ "I have went to school."
✅ "I have gone to school."

📖 Explanation: After "have", use past participle (V3) – "gone" not "went".

❌ "He go to school every day."
✅ "He goes to school every day."

📖 Explanation: Third person singular (he/she/it) needs -s/-es in present tense.

❌ "I am knowing the answer."
✅ "I know the answer."

📖 Explanation: Stative verbs (know, believe, like) don't take continuous form.

🎯 Tips for BCS, IELTS & Bank Job Tense Questions

  • Look for time markers – words like "yesterday", "now", "tomorrow" tell you which tense to use.
  • Watch for signal words – "for" and "since" often indicate perfect or perfect continuous tenses.
  • Remember irregular verbs – go → went → gone, write → wrote → written, etc.
  • Practice regularly – the more you practice, the faster you'll identify the correct tense.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How many English tenses are there? ▾
There are 12 English tenses: Present (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous), Past (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous), and Future (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous). Our quiz covers all of them.

What is the most common tense mistake in English? ▾
The most common mistakes are mixing up present and past tenses, forgetting to add -s for third person singular in present tense, and using the wrong form after auxiliary verbs (e.g., using V2 instead of V3 after 'have').
