What is the difference between a noun and a pronoun?
A noun is the name of a person, place, thing, or idea (Rahim, Dhaka, book). A pronoun replaces a noun to avoid repetition (he, she, they, it).
What is Subject-Verb Agreement?
Subject-Verb Agreement means that the verb must match the subject in number (singular or plural) and person. If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular; if plural, the verb must be plural.
How many tenses are there in English?
There are 12 tenses in English: Present (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous), Past (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous), and Future (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous).
What is the difference between active and passive voice?
Active voice focuses on the subject doing the action (He writes a letter). Passive voice focuses on the action itself (A letter is written by him).
What is the difference between direct and indirect speech?
Direct speech quotes the exact words spoken (He said, 'I am happy'). Indirect speech reports what was said (He said that he was happy).
What are the 8 parts of speech?
The 8 parts of speech are: Noun, Pronoun, Verb, Adjective, Adverb, Preposition, Conjunction, and Interjection. Each plays a different role in a sentence.
What is the rule for 'a' and 'an'?
Use 'a' before words that start with a consonant sound (a book, a university). Use 'an' before words that start with a vowel sound (an apple, an hour). The rule depends on sound, not spelling.
