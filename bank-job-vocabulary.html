Bank Job English Vocabulary – 100 Most Important Words for Bank Exams

📅 July 2, 2026 · ⏱️ 8 min read · 📚 Bank Job Preparation

The English section in Bangladesh Bank job exams is a decisive factor for thousands of candidates every year. Bank job exams – including Bangladesh Bank, Sonali Bank, Janata Bank, Agrani Bank, and other commercial banks – heavily test vocabulary, synonyms, antonyms, idioms, and reading comprehension.

In this comprehensive guide, we have compiled 100 of the most frequently tested English words from previous bank job exams. Each word includes Bangla meaning, synonyms, antonyms, and example sentences to help you master them quickly.

📌 Did you know? Bank job written exams in Bangladesh typically have 40–50 English MCQ questions to be completed in 30–45 minutes. Vocabulary accounts for approximately 40% of these questions.

Why Vocabulary is Critical for Bank Jobs

  • High weightage: Approximately 40% of the English questions test vocabulary directly.
  • Synonyms and Antonyms: These are the most common question types in bank English.
  • Reading Comprehension: A strong vocabulary helps you understand passages faster and answer more accurately.
  • Bank-specific terms: Banking, finance, and economics vocabulary is frequently tested.

How to Use This Vocabulary List

  1. Learn 10 words daily – consistency beats cramming.
  2. Use flashcards – spaced repetition helps retain words long‑term.
  3. Practice with mock tests – test your knowledge regularly.
  4. Review weak areas – focus on words you find difficult.

Bank Exam English Preparation Strategy

1. Daily Vocabulary Practice

Dedicate 15–20 minutes daily to learning new words. Use flashcards and spaced repetition for long-term retention. Apps like Ovidhan offer smart flashcards with SM-2 algorithm to help you remember words effectively.

2. Read Financial News Daily

Reading financial and business news enhances both vocabulary and the ability to express ideas effectively. Focus on The Daily Star Business section, The Financial Express, and other financial publications.

3. Practice Grammar and Vocabulary Together

Revise one grammar topic each day while simultaneously learning new vocabulary words. This integrated approach improves overall English proficiency.

4. Take Mock Tests

Practice with 10,000+ MCQ questions to simulate exam conditions. Regular testing helps identify weak areas and builds confidence.

Common Bank Exam Vocabulary Question Types

  • Synonyms: Find the closest meaning of a given word.
  • Antonyms: Identify the opposite meaning.
  • One‑word Substitutions: Replace a phrase with a single word.
  • Idioms and Phrases: Understand the meaning of common expressions.
  • Sentence Completion: Fill in blanks with appropriate vocabulary.
