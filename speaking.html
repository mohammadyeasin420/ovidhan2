🗣️ 1. Introduction – Why Speaking is Different

Let me share something personal. When I first started learning English, I could read and write well. But when I tried to speak, my mind went blank. The words were in my head, but they wouldn't come out. I was terrified of making mistakes. I was embarrassed by my accent. I felt like everyone was judging me. (যখন আমি প্রথম ইংরেজি শিখতে শুরু করি, আমি ভালো পড়তে এবং লিখতে পারতাম। কিন্তু যখন আমি কথা বলার চেষ্টা করতাম, আমার মাথা খালি হয়ে যেত। শব্দগুলি আমার মাথায় ছিল, কিন্তু বের হত না। আমি ভুল করার ভয়ে terrified ছিলাম। আমি আমার উচ্চারণে embarrassed ছিলাম।)

Speaking is different from reading or writing. It requires real-time thinking, physical mouth movements, and confidence. You can't look up a word when you're speaking. You can't edit your sentence. You just have to speak. (কথা বলা পড়া বা লেখার থেকে আলাদা। এর জন্য প্রয়োজন রিয়েল-টাইম চিন্তা, শারীরিক মুখের নড়াচড়া এবং আত্মবিশ্বাস। কথা বলার সময় আপনি শব্দ খুঁজে দেখতে পারেন না। আপনি আপনার বাক্য সম্পাদনা করতে পারেন না। আপনাকে শুধু কথা বলতে হবে।)

In this guide, I will teach you how to speak English—not just grammar rules, but practical techniques. You will learn how to improve your pronunciation, how to speak fluently, and how to avoid common mistakes. For daily practice exercises, visit our Practice Hub. (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে কীভাবে ইংরেজি বলতে হয় শেখাব—শুধু ব্যাকরণের নিয়ম নয়, বরং ব্যবহারিক কৌশল।)

💡 A Lesson I'll Never Forget

The day I stopped worrying about perfect grammar and started speaking was the day I finally improved. Native speakers don't expect perfection — they expect communication. Just start speaking.

🔊 2. Pronunciation Guide – Sound Like a Native

Pronunciation is the key to being understood. Here are the most common pronunciation challenges for Bangladeshi learners and how to fix them. (উচ্চারণ হল বোঝার চাবিকাঠি। এখানে বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীদের জন্য সবচেয়ে সাধারণ উচ্চারণ চ্যালেঞ্জ এবং সেগুলি কীভাবে ঠিক করবেন তা দেওয়া হলো।)

🔴 Sound: /θ/ and /ð/ (Th-sound)

These sounds do not exist in Bangla. Many learners pronounce them as /t/ or /d/.

Think /θɪŋk/
চিন্তা করা
/θɪŋk/
This /ðɪs/
এই
/ðɪs/
💡 How to Fix

Put your tongue between your teeth and blow air. For /θ/ (think), push air out. For /ð/ (this), use your voice. Practice: "Three things I think about." "These are those."

🔴 Sound: /v/ (V-sound)

Many Bangladeshi learners pronounce /v/ as /b/ or /w/.

Very /ˈver.i/
খুব
/ˈver.i/
Village /ˈvɪl.ɪdʒ/
গ্রাম
/ˈvɪl.ɪdʒ/
💡 How to Fix

Bite your bottom lip lightly and blow air. Practice: "Very valuable village." "I love you very much."

🔴 Sound: /r/ and /l/

Bangla has different r and l sounds. English r and l are distinct.

Right /raɪt/
সঠিক
/raɪt/
Light /laɪt/
আলো
/laɪt/
💡 How to Fix

For /r/, curl your tongue back without touching the roof of your mouth. For /l/, touch the ridge behind your teeth. Practice: "Right and light are different."

🧠 Daily Pronunciation Practice

Practice shadowing — listen to a native speaker and repeat immediately. Use YouTube videos, podcasts, or the dialogues in this guide. Do this for 5 minutes every day.

🚀 3. Fluency Techniques – Speak Smoothly

Fluency is about speaking smoothly and confidently. Here are the techniques that actually work. (সাবলীলতা হল মসৃণ এবং আত্মবিশ্বাসের সাথে কথা বলা। এখানে আসলেই কাজ করে এমন কৌশল দেওয়া হলো।)

1. Shadowing
Listen to a native speaker and repeat what they say immediately — at the same time. This trains your mouth and ears to work together. Do this for 5 minutes daily.
💡 Use YouTube videos, podcasts, or English movies with subtitles.
2. Think in English
Stop translating from Bangla to English in your head. Start thinking directly in English. Describe your day in English, even if only in your head. It feels slow at first, but it gets faster.
💡 Practice: "I am going to the kitchen to drink water."
3. Record Yourself
Record yourself speaking English. Listen back. You'll hear your mistakes and improve faster. It's uncomfortable at first, but it works better than any other technique.
💡 Read a dialogue from this guide and record it. Compare with the original.
4. Use Fillers (Strategically)
Native speakers use fillers like "um," "well," "you know," and "actually." They give you time to think and sound more natural. Don't overuse them, but use them.
💡 "Well, I think..." "You know, what I mean is..."
5. Speak Slowly
Speaking slowly is better than speaking fast with mistakes. Slow down. Give yourself time to think. Native speakers will understand you better.
💡 Practice: "I... would... like... to... order... some... water."
🧠 The Secret to Fluency

Fluency is not about speed. It is about the ability to communicate without hesitation. Focus on being understood, not on speaking fast. Speed comes naturally with practice.

💬 4. Real-Life Dialogues – Practice with Context

Here are real dialogues for common situations. Read them aloud, practice with a partner, and shadow the speaker. (এখানে সাধারণ পরিস্থিতির জন্য বাস্তব সংলাপ দেওয়া হলো। সেগুলি জোরে পড়ুন, একজন সঙ্গীর সাথে অনুশীলন করুন এবং স্পিকারের ছায়া অনুসরণ করুন।)

🍽️ At a Restaurant

👨 Waiter: Good evening! Welcome to our restaurant. What would you like to order?
শুভ সন্ধ্যা! আমাদের রেস্তোরাঁয় স্বাগতম। আপনি কী অর্ডার করতে চান?

🙋 You: I'd like the chicken biryani, please. And could I have a glass of water?
আমি মুরগির বিরিয়ানি চাই, দয়া করে। এবং আমি এক গ্লাস পানি পেতে পারি?

👨 Waiter: Of course. Anything else?
অবশ্যই। আর কিছু?

🙋 You: That's all, thank you.
এটুকুই, ধন্যবাদ।

🎤 Job Interview

👨 Interviewer: Tell me about yourself.
আপনার সম্পর্কে বলুন।

🙋 You: I'm a recent graduate with a passion for marketing. I've completed internships at two companies and increased social media engagement by 30%. I'm excited about this opportunity.
আমি একজন সাম্প্রতিক স্নাতক, মার্কেটিংয়ে আগ্রহী। আমি দুটি কোম্পানিতে ইন্টার্নশিপ সম্পন্ন করেছি এবং সোশ্যাল মিডিয়া এনগেজমেন্ট ৩০% বাড়িয়েছি। আমি এই সুযোগের জন্য উত্সাহিত।

👨 Interviewer: Why do you want to work here?
আপনি কেন এখানে কাজ করতে চান?

🙋 You: I admire your company's innovative approach. I believe my skills can contribute to your team's success.
আমি আপনার কোম্পানির উদ্ভাবনী পদ্ধতির প্রশংসা করি। আমি বিশ্বাস করি আমার দক্ষতা আপনার দলের সাফল্যে অবদান রাখতে পারে।

✈️ At the Airport

👨 Officer: Good morning. May I see your passport and ticket?
সুপ্রভাত। আমি কি আপনার পাসপোর্ট এবং টিকিট দেখতে পারি?

🙋 You: Certainly. Here they are.
অবশ্যই। এই নিন।

👨 Officer: Where are you traveling to today?
আপনি আজ কোথায় ভ্রমণ করছেন?

🙋 You: I'm flying to London.
আমি লন্ডনে যাচ্ছি।

👨 Officer: Do you have any checked baggage?
আপনার কোনো চেক করা লাগেজ আছে?

🙋 You: Yes, one bag.
হ্যাঁ, একটি ব্যাগ।

⚠️ 5. Common Mistakes – What Bangladeshi Learners Say Wrong

These are the most common speaking mistakes made by Bangladeshi learners. Avoid them and sound more natural. (এগুলি বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীদের করা সবচেয়ে সাধারণ কথা বলার ভুল। এগুলি এড়িয়ে চলুন এবং আরও স্বাভাবিক শোনান।)

❌ I am agree.
✅ I agree.
💡 In Bangla, "রাজি" is an adjective, so we say "আমি রাজি." But in English, "agree" is a verb—no "am" needed.
❌ I am knowing him.
✅ I know him.
💡 "Know" is a stative verb. It describes a state, not an action. Use simple present, not continuous.
❌ I wake up at morning.
✅ I wake up in the morning.
💡 Use "in" for parts of the day: in the morning, in the afternoon, in the evening.
❌ I am going to office.
✅ I am going to the office.
💡 Use "the" before specific places like office, hospital, school.
❌ I have a doubt.
✅ I have a question.
💡 "Doubt" means uncertainty. "Question" means you want to ask something.

💡 6. Speaking Tips – Build Confidence

Confidence is the biggest factor in speaking. Here are practical tips to build your confidence. (আত্মবিশ্বাসই কথা বলার সবচেয়ে বড় বিষয়। এখানে আপনার আত্মবিশ্বাস বাড়ানোর জন্য ব্যবহারিক টিপস দেওয়া হলো।)

1. Start with Simple Sentences
Don't try to speak long, complex sentences. Start with short, simple sentences. "I am happy." "I like tea." As you gain confidence, make them longer.
2. Practice with Friends
Find a friend or classmate who also wants to learn English. Practice together. It's less intimidating than speaking to a stranger.
3. Speak to Yourself
Describe what you are doing in English. "I am cooking rice." "I am going to the market." This helps you practice without pressure.
4. Accept Mistakes
Everyone makes mistakes — including native speakers. When you make a mistake, correct it and move on. Don't let fear of mistakes stop you.
5. Listen More Than You Speak
Listen to native speakers. Notice how they say things. Mimic their pronunciation, rhythm, and intonation. The more you listen, the better you speak.
🧠 Confidence Trick

Remember: Every native English speaker was once a learner. They made mistakes too. Your accent is a sign of your hard work, not a weakness. Be proud of your progress.

❓ 8. Frequently Asked Questions

How can I learn to speak English fluently? ▾
Learn by practicing pronunciation, shadowing native speakers, thinking in English, and using real-life dialogues. This guide gives you all the techniques you need.
What is the best way to improve English pronunciation? ▾
Practice sounds that don't exist in Bangla, like 'th' and 'v'. Use shadowing and record yourself. We provide specific pronunciation tips in this guide.
Why do Bangladeshi learners make 'I am agree' mistakes? ▾
In Bangla, 'রাজি' is an adjective, so we say 'আমি রাজি.' But in English, 'agree' is a verb—no 'am' needed. We explain this and other common mistakes in this guide.
How do I practice speaking English at home? ▾
Read aloud, record yourself, shadow native speakers, and use the scenario guides. For daily practice exercises, visit our Practice Hub.
