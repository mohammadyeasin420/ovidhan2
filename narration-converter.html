📚 Direct vs Indirect Speech

Understanding direct and indirect speech (narration) is essential for BCS, IELTS, HSC, SSC, Bank Jobs, and university admission. (Direct এবং Indirect Speech (Narration) বোঝা BCS, IELTS, HSC, SSC, ব্যাংক জব এবং বিশ্ববিদ্যালয় ভর্তির জন্য অত্যন্ত গুরুত্বপূর্ণ।)

📌 Direct Speech

Direct speech repeats the exact words of the speaker using quotation marks. (Direct speech বক্তার ঠিক কথাগুলো উদ্ধৃতি চিহ্ন সহ পুনরাবৃত্তি করে।)

📝 She said, "I am tired."
সে বলল, "আমি ক্লান্ত।"

📌 Indirect Speech

Indirect speech reports the meaning without quotation marks, changing pronouns, tenses, and time expressions. (Indirect speech উদ্ধৃতি চিহ্ন ছাড়া অর্থটি জানায়, সর্বনাম, কাল এবং সময়ের অভিব্যক্তি পরিবর্তন করে।)

📝 She said that she was tired.
সে বলল যে সে ক্লান্ত ছিল।

What is the difference between direct and indirect speech? ▾
Direct speech repeats the exact words of the speaker using quotation marks (e.g., 'I am tired,' she said). Indirect speech reports the meaning without quotation marks, changing pronouns, tenses, and time expressions (e.g., She said that she was tired).
Can this tool help with BCS and IELTS preparation? ▾

What are the rules for changing tense in narration? ▾
When converting direct to indirect, present tenses usually change to past tenses (Present → Past, Present Perfect → Past Perfect). Past tenses often change to Past Perfect. Future tenses change to Conditional (will → would).
