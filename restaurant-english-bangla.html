🍽️ 1. Introduction – Why Restaurant English Matters

Think about this: you are in a restaurant in London, New York, or even at a five-star hotel in Dhaka. The waiter comes to your table and asks, "What would you like to order?" Your heart starts beating fast. You know what you want, but you don't know how to say it in English. You point at the menu and mumble something. The waiter looks confused. You feel embarrassed. (একটা কথা ভাবুন: আপনি লন্ডন, নিউ ইয়র্ক বা ঢাকার একটি পাঁচ তারকা হোটেলের রেস্তোরাঁয় আছেন। ওয়েটার আপনার টেবিলে এসে জিজ্ঞেস করে, "আপনি কী অর্ডার করতে চান?" আপনার হৃদয় দ্রুত স্পন্দন শুরু করে। আপনি জানেন আপনি কী চান, কিন্তু ইংরেজিতে কীভাবে বলবেন তা জানেন না। আপনি মেনুর দিকে ইশারা করে কিছু বলছেন। ওয়েটার বিভ্রান্ত দেখায়। আপনি বিব্রত বোধ করছেন।)

This is a common experience for many Bangladeshi learners. Restaurant English is one of the most practical and high-stakes situations you will face. Whether you are traveling, working abroad, or just dining at a fancy restaurant, knowing how to order food in English is essential.

In this guide, I will teach you everything you need to know – from walking into the restaurant to paying the bill. I have included real conversations and Bangla translations for every sentence. I have also added cultural tips that will help you understand the etiquette of dining in English-speaking countries. By the end of this guide, you will feel confident ordering food in any restaurant, anywhere in the world. (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে রেস্তোরাঁয় খাবার অর্ডার করার সবকিছু শেখাব। আমি বাস্তব কথোপকথন এবং বাংলা অনুবাদ অন্তর্ভুক্ত করেছি।)

💡 A Personal Story

I promised myself that I would learn proper restaurant English. And now, I want to help you avoid the same embarrassment. You can do this!

📝 2. Ordering Food – The Most Important Skill

Here are the most common sentences used to order food in a restaurant. Practice these and you will never feel nervous again.

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি মেনু দেখতে পারি?
📝 Literal: I can see the menu?
🗣️ Natural: Can I see the menu, please?
💡 Always add "please" to sound polite. This is a basic but crucial phrase.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি মুরগির বিরিয়ানি অর্ডার করতে চাই।
📝 Literal: I want to order chicken biryani.
🗣️ Natural: I'd like the chicken biryani, please.
💡 "I'd like" is a contraction of "I would like" – it's more polite than "I want."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনি কি সুপারিশ করবেন?
📝 Literal: What will you recommend?
🗣️ Natural: What do you recommend?
💡 This is a great question to ask if you don't know what to order. Waiters love to help!
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি নিরামিষ খাবার চাই।
📝 Literal: I want vegetarian food.
🗣️ Natural: I'd like something vegetarian, please.
💡 "Something vegetarian" is more natural than "vegetarian food" – it sounds less robotic.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এক প্লেট ভুনা খিচুড়ি অর্ডার করব।
📝 Literal: I will order one plate of bhuna khichuri.
🗣️ Natural: I'll have a plate of bhuna khichuri, please.
💡 "I'll have" is very common in restaurants. It's short, natural, and polite.
🇧🇩 Bangla: এই খাবারটি কতক্ষণ লাগবে?
📝 Literal: How much time will this food take?
🗣️ Natural: How long will it take?
💡 "How long" is the standard way to ask about time. "It" refers to the food.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি অতিরিক্ত রুটি পেতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I get extra bread?
🗣️ Natural: Could I have some extra bread, please?
💡 "Could I have" is even more polite than "Can I get." Use it for extra requests.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এই খাবারটি নিয়ে যেতে চাই।
📝 Literal: I want to take this food.
🗣️ Natural: I'd like this to go, please.
💡 "To go" means takeaway. In British English, you might also hear "takeaway."
🧠 Golden Rule for Ordering

Always use "I would like" or "I'll have" instead of "I want." "I want" sounds demanding. "I would like" sounds polite and natural. Practice it until it becomes a habit.

🥤 3. Ordering Drinks – Stay Hydrated

Drinks are often ordered separately from food. Here are the most common phrases for ordering beverages. (পানীয় সাধারণত খাবার থেকে আলাদাভাবে অর্ডার করা হয়। পানীয় অর্ডারের জন্য সবচেয়ে সাধারণ বাক্য এখানে দেওয়া হলো।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এক গ্লাস পানি চাই।
📝 Literal: I want one glass of water.
🗣️ Natural: Can I have a glass of water, please?
💡 "Can I have" is the most natural way to ask for a drink.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এক কাপ চা অর্ডার করব।
📝 Literal: I will order one cup of tea.
🗣️ Natural: I'll have a cup of tea, please.
💡 "I'll have" works for drinks too. It's short and natural.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি ঠান্ডা পানীয় পেতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I get cold drink?
🗣️ Natural: Could I have something cold to drink?
💡 "Something cold to drink" is more natural than "cold drink" – it sounds more conversational.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনার কি বোতলজাত পানি আছে?
📝 Literal: Do you have bottled water?
🗣️ Natural: Do you have bottled water?
💡 This one is the same in literal and natural English! Simple and effective.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি আরও পানি পেতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I get more water?
🗣️ Natural: Could I have some more water, please?
💡 "Some more water" is more natural than "more water." Always add "please."

❓ 4. Asking Questions – Get the Information You Need

Sometimes you need more information before ordering. Here are the most common questions to ask the waiter. (কখনও কখনও অর্ডার করার আগে আপনার আরও তথ্যের প্রয়োজন হয়। ওয়েটারকে জিজ্ঞাসা করার জন্য এখানে সবচেয়ে সাধারণ প্রশ্ন দেওয়া হলো।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: এই খাবারে কী কী আছে?
📝 Literal: What is in this food?
🗣️ Natural: What's in this dish?
💡 "Dish" is a more natural word for "food" in a restaurant context.
🇧🇩 Bangla: এটি কি মশলাদার?
📝 Literal: Is it spicy?
🗣️ Natural: Is it spicy?
💡 This one is the same in literal and natural English. Very useful!
🇧🇩 Bangla: এতে কি মাংস আছে?
📝 Literal: Does it have meat?
🗣️ Natural: Does it contain meat?
💡 "Contain" is slightly more formal and polite than "have" in this context.
🇧🇩 Bangla: এটি কি নিরামিষ?
📝 Literal: Is it vegetarian?
🗣️ Natural: Is it vegetarian?
💡 Again, this one is the same. Simple and direct.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনি কি সুপারিশ করবেন?
📝 Literal: What will you recommend?
🗣️ Natural: What would you recommend?
💡 "Would" is more polite than "will." This is a great question to ask.

💰 5. Asking for the Bill – It's Time to Pay

After you finish your meal, you need to ask for the bill. Here is how to do it politely. (খাবার শেষ করার পর, আপনাকে বিল চাইতে হবে। এখানে তা কীভাবে করবেন তা দেওয়া হলো।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি বিল চাই।
📝 Literal: I want the bill.
🗣️ Natural: Can I have the bill, please?
💡 "Can I have" is much more polite than "I want." In American English, you can also say "check."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি ক্যাশে দিতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I pay in cash?
🗣️ Natural: Can I pay by cash?
💡 "By cash" is slightly more natural than "in cash." You can also say "Can I pay with cash?"
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি কার্ডে দিতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I pay by card?
🗣️ Natural: Can I pay by card?
💡 This one is the same. You can also say "Can I pay with a credit card?"
🇧🇩 Bangla: বিলে কি সার্ভিস চার্জ আছে?
📝 Literal: Is there service charge in the bill?
🗣️ Natural: Is there a service charge?
💡 You don't need to say "in the bill" – "service charge" is understood in the context.
🇧🇩 Bangla: বিলটি আলাদা করে দিতে পারেন?
📝 Literal: Can you give the bill separately?
🗣️ Natural: Can we have separate bills, please?
💡 This is very useful when dining with friends. "Separate bills" means each person pays for their own food.

😤 6. Complaints & Problems – How to Handle Issues

Sometimes things go wrong. The food might be cold, or you might get the wrong order. Here is how to complain politely without being rude. (কখনও কখনও কিছু ভুল হতে পারে। খাবার ঠান্ডা হতে পারে, বা আপনি ভুল অর্ডার পেতে পারেন। অভদ্র না হয়ে কীভাবে অভিযোগ করবেন তা এখানে দেওয়া হলো।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এটি অর্ডার করিনি।
📝 Literal: I didn't order this.
🗣️ Natural: Excuse me, I didn't order this.
💡 Always start with "Excuse me" to get the waiter's attention politely.
🇧🇩 Bangla: এই খাবারটি ঠান্ডা।
📝 Literal: This food is cold.
🗣️ Natural: I'm sorry, but this is cold.
💡 "I'm sorry" softens the complaint and makes it sound less aggressive.
🇧🇩 Bangla: এটি খুব মশলাদার।
📝 Literal: It is very spicy.
🗣️ Natural: This is too spicy for me.
💡 Adding "for me" makes it personal and less critical of the food itself.
🇧🇩 Bangla: এখানে একটি সমস্যা আছে।
📝 Literal: There is a problem here.
🗣️ Natural: I'm afraid there's a small problem.
💡 "I'm afraid" is a polite way to introduce a problem. It shows you are not trying to be difficult.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এটি ফেরত দিতে চাই।
📝 Literal: I want to return this.
🗣️ Natural: I'm sorry, but I'd like to send this back.
💡 "Send back" is the natural phrase. "Return" sounds like you are returning a product to a store.
💡 How to Complain Like a Pro

When you complain, always start with a polite phrase like "Excuse me" or "I'm sorry." State the problem clearly and end with a solution – for example, "Could you heat this up, please?" Restaurants want you to be happy, so most will fix the problem immediately.

💬 8. Real Dialogues – Ordering in Action

Here are three real restaurant conversations. Read them aloud and practice with a friend. (এখানে তিনটি বাস্তব রেস্তোরাঁয় কথোপকথন দেওয়া হলো। সেগুলি জোরে পড়ুন এবং একজন বন্ধুর সাথে অনুশীলন করুন।)

🟡 Dialogue 1: Ordering a Meal

Waiter: Good evening! Welcome to our restaurant. What would you like to order?
শুভ সন্ধ্যা! আমাদের রেস্তোরাঁয় স্বাগতম। আপনি কী অর্ডার করতে চান?

You: Hello! I'd like the chicken biryani, please. And can I have a glass of water?
হ্যালো! আমি মুরগির বিরিয়ানি চাই, দয়া করে। এবং আমি এক গ্লাস পানি পেতে পারি?

Waiter: Of course. Anything else?
অবশ্যই। আর কিছু?

You: That's all, thank you.
এটুকুই, ধন্যবাদ।

🔴 Dialogue 2: Complaining Politely

You: Excuse me?
আমি কি একটু বলতে পারি?

Waiter: Yes, how can I help you?
জি, আমি কীভাবে সাহায্য করতে পারি?

You: I'm sorry, but this biryani is cold. Could you heat it up, please?
আমি দুঃখিত, কিন্তু এই বিরিয়ানি ঠান্ডা। আপনি কি এটি গরম করে দিতে পারেন?

Waiter: I apologize for that. I'll get you a fresh one right away.
আমি এর জন্য দুঃখিত। আমি এখনই আপনাকে একটি নতুন দিচ্ছি।

You: Thank you very much.
আপনাকে অনেক ধন্যবাদ।

🟢 Dialogue 3: Asking for the Bill

You: Can I have the bill, please?
আমি কি বিল পেতে পারি, দয়া করে?

Waiter: Certainly. Here you are.
অবশ্যই। এই নিন।

You: Can I pay by card?
আমি কি কার্ডে দিতে পারি?

Waiter: Yes, we accept all major cards. I'll bring the card machine.
হ্যাঁ, আমরা সব বড় কার্ড গ্রহণ করি। আমি কার্ড মেশিন নিয়ে আসছি।

You: Thank you. Have a good day.
ধন্যবাদ। আপনার দিন ভালো হোক।

🌍 9. Cultural Tips – Dining Etiquette in English-Speaking Countries

Knowing the language is important, but knowing the culture is just as important. Here are some cultural tips for dining in English-speaking countries. (ভাষা জানা গুরুত্বপূর্ণ, কিন্তু সংস্কৃতি জানাও সমান গুরুত্বপূর্ণ। এখানে ইংরেজি-ভাষী দেশগুলিতে খাবার খাওয়ার জন্য কিছু সাংস্কৃতিক টিপস দেওয়া হলো।)

💵 Tipping
In the US and Canada, tipping is expected – usually 15-20% of the bill. In the UK and Europe, tipping is optional but appreciated (10% is common). In Bangladesh, a small tip is appreciated but not required.
🤝 Calling the Waiter
Never shout "Waiter!" – it's considered rude. Instead, make eye contact and raise your hand slightly, or say "Excuse me" politely.
🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Sharing Food
In Western restaurants, sharing food is not as common as in Bangladesh. If you want to share, ask your dining companions first.
🍽️ Table Manners
Keep your elbows off the table while eating. Wait for everyone to be served before you start eating. Say "thank you" when your food arrives.

📘 10. Grammar Focus – Polite Requests

In restaurants, you will mostly use polite requests. Here is how to make them correctly. (রেস্তোরাঁয়, আপনি বেশিরভাগই পলিট রিকোয়েস্ট ব্যবহার করবেন। এখানে কীভাবে সেগুলি সঠিকভাবে তৈরি করবেন তা দেওয়া হলো।)

Modal Verbs
Use would, could, can for polite requests.
Examples
I would like the biryani. / Could I have some water? / Can I have the bill?
More Formal
Could is more formal than can. Use would for preferences.
Always Add "Please"
Adding "please" makes any request more polite. Always use it in a restaurant.
🧠 Quick Memory Trick

Remember: "Would" for preferences, "Could" for requests, "Can" for simple questions. Always add "please" at the end. It sounds natural and polite.

⚠️ 11. Common Mistakes – Restaurant English

Avoid these common errors that come from direct Bangla translation.

❌ Wrong (Literal) ✅ Correct (Natural) 💡 Why?
I want biryani. I'd like the biryani, please. "I want" is too direct. "I'd like" is polite and natural.
Give me water. Can I have some water, please? "Give me" sounds like a command. "Can I have" is polite.
What is the price? How much is this? "How much" is the standard phrase for asking about cost.
This food is not good. I'm afraid this isn't quite right. "Not good" sounds like a complaint. "Isn't quite right" is more polite.
I am wanting the bill. Could I have the bill, please? "Wanting" is not used in this way. Use "I would like" or "Could I have."

❓ 13. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I order food in English at a restaurant? ▾
Start with "I would like..." or "Can I have..." For example: "I would like the chicken biryani, please." or "Can I have a glass of water?" Always say "please" and "thank you" to be polite.
How do I ask for the bill in English? ▾
You say "Can I have the bill, please?" or "Could I get the check, please?" In British English, they say "bill," and in American English, they say "check." Both are correct.
How do I complain about food in a restaurant? ▾
Be polite but clear. Say "Excuse me, this is not what I ordered" or "I'm sorry, but this is too salty/cold." Restaurants want you to be happy, so they will usually fix the problem.
What is the difference between "would like" and "want"? ▾
"Would like" is more polite and formal. "Want" is more direct and casual. In a restaurant, always use "would like" or "can I have" to be polite.
How do I say "I am a vegetarian" in English? ▾
You say "I am a vegetarian." If you eat fish but not meat, say "I am a pescatarian." If you don't eat anything from animals, say "I am a vegan."
