📚 Understanding English Articles

Articles are small words that come before nouns. They tell us if the noun is specific or general. Understanding articles is essential for BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs, HSC, and university admission exams. (Articles হলো ছোট শব্দ যা বিশেষ্যের আগে বসে। তারা বলে যে বিশেষ্যটি নির্দিষ্ট নাকি সাধারণ।)

📌 The Three Types of Articles

Type Article Example Bangla Usage
Definite the The sun is bright. সূর্য উজ্জ্বল। Specific noun (known to the reader)
Indefinite a, an I saw a dog. আমি একটি কুকুর দেখলাম। General noun (not specific)
Zero Article — Dogs are friendly. কুকুর বন্ধুত্বপূর্ণ। Plural/general statements

📌 Key Rules for Articles

  • Use "a" before words that start with a consonant sound: a book, a university, a one-eyed man.
  • Use "an" before words that start with a vowel sound: an apple, an hour, an MBA.
  • Use "the" for unique objects (the sun, the moon), superlatives (the best), and when both speaker and listener know what is being referred to.
  • Use zero article for plural countable nouns in general (Dogs are friendly), uncountable nouns in general (Water is essential), proper nouns (Dhaka), languages, sports, and meals.

❌ Common Article Mistakes

❌ "She is a honest girl."
✅ "She is an honest girl."

📖 Explanation: "Honest" has a silent 'h' – use "an" before vowel sounds.

❌ "I have a few water."
✅ "I have a little water."

📖 Explanation: "Water" is uncountable – use "a little" not "a few".

❌ "I love the nature."
✅ "I love nature."

📖 Explanation: Abstract nouns in general take zero article.

🎯 Tips for BCS, IELTS & Bank Job Article Questions

  • Listen to the sound – "a" vs "an" depends on pronunciation, not spelling.
  • Look for uniqueness – if something is one of a kind (sun, moon), use "the".
  • Check if it's countable/uncountable – uncountable nouns in general take zero article.
  • Remember exceptions – "the United States", "the Philippines" take "the" because they are plural.

What is the difference between 'a' and 'an'? ▾
Use 'a' before words that start with a consonant sound (a book, a university) and 'an' before words that start with a vowel sound (an apple, an hour). The sound matters, not just the letter.
When do we use zero article (no article)? ▾
We use zero article with: plural countable nouns in general (Dogs are friendly), uncountable nouns in general (Water is essential), proper nouns (Dhaka, John), languages (English), sports (cricket), and meals (breakfast).
