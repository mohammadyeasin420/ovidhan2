📖 Introduction: What Are "Have" Collocations?

The verb "have" is one of the most versatile words in English. We use it to talk about experiences, meals, health, relationships, and things we own. ("Have" ক্রিয়াপদটি ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে বহুমুখী শব্দগুলির মধ্যে একটি। আমরা এটি অভিজ্ঞতা, খাবার, স্বাস্থ্য, সম্পর্ক এবং আমাদের মালিকানাধীন জিনিস সম্পর্কে কথা বলতে ব্যবহার করি।)

Think about your daily routine — you have breakfast, you have a shower (or a bath!), you have a conversation with a friend. 'Have' is everywhere! (আপনার দৈনন্দিন রুটিন সম্পর্কে চিন্তা করুন — আপনি সকালের নাস্তা করেন, আপনি গোসল করেন, আপনি বন্ধুর সাথে কথোপকথন করেন। 'Have' সর্বত্র আছে!)

But here's the catch — we say "have breakfast", but we say "take a shower" in American English. Confusing, right? Don't worry, I'll clear it all up for you in this guide. (কিন্তু এখানে একটি জটিলতা আছে — আমরা "have breakfast" বলি, কিন্তু আমেরিকান ইংরেজিতে আমরা "take a shower" বলি। বিভ্রান্তিকর, তাই না? চিন্তা করবেন না, আমি এই গাইডে সব পরিষ্কার করে দেব।)

📝 Think about it: "I have a headache." Sounds natural, right?
📝 ভেবে দেখুন: "I have a headache." স্বাভাবিক শোনায়, তাই না?
❌ "I make a headache." Now that sounds completely wrong!
❌ "I make a headache." এখন এটি সম্পূর্ণ ভুল শোনায়!

In this guide, I'll walk you through 50+ "have" collocations organised by meaning, with Bangla translations and example sentences. Let's dive in! (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে ৫০+ "have" কলোকেশন শেখাবো, বাংলা অর্থ এবং উদাহরণ সহ। শুরু করা যাক!)

❌ Common Mistakes with "Have" Collocations

Many students confuse "have" with "make", "do", or "take". Let's look at the most common errors. (অনেক শিক্ষার্থী "have" কে "make", "do" বা "take" এর সাথে গুলিয়ে ফেলে। আসুন সবচেয়ে সাধারণ ভুলগুলি দেখি।)

❌ "I made breakfast for everyone." (This is actually correct, but let's check the next one)
✅ "I had breakfast." (Not "I made breakfast" unless you cooked it)

📖 Explanation: "Have breakfast" means you ate it. "Make breakfast" means you cooked it.

❌ "I take breakfast at 8 AM." (In American English, this sounds unnatural)
✅ "I have breakfast at 8 AM."

📖 Explanation: For meals, we usually use "have" in British English and "eat" or "have" in American English. "Take" is rare.

❌ "I have a shower." (This is correct in British English)
✅ "I take a shower." (More common in American English)

📖 Explanation: Both are correct. British English prefers "have a shower", American English prefers "take a shower".

❌ "I'm having a problem with this." (Wait, this is actually correct!)
✅ "I have a problem." (For simple present, use "have")

📖 Explanation: Use simple present for permanent states. Use continuous for temporary situations.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the meaning of "have collocations"? ▾
Have collocations are combinations where the verb "have" is used with a noun to describe experiences, meals, ownership, or interactions. For example, "have breakfast" means to eat breakfast. These are essential for daily English.
Why are "have" collocations important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
"Have" collocations appear frequently in error correction, sentence completion, and vocabulary sections of BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Mastering them boosts your score.
What is the difference between "have" and "take" collocations? ▾
"Have" is often used for meals, experiences, and ownership (have breakfast, have fun). "Take" is used for actions, time, and transportation (take a shower, take a bus). British English often uses "have a shower" while American English uses "take a shower".
How can I remember "have" collocations? ▾
Group them by category: meals (have breakfast), experiences (have fun), health (have a headache), and relationships (have a conversation). Practice using them in your daily conversations.
