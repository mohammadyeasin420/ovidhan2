📖 Introduction: What Are "Make" Collocations?

"Make" is one of the most versatile verbs in English. We use it when we create, produce, or cause something. ("Make" ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে বহুমুখী ক্রিয়াপদগুলির মধ্যে একটি। আমরা এটি ব্যবহার করি যখন আমরা কিছু তৈরি করি, উৎপাদন করি বা ঘটাই।)

However, "make" doesn't go with every word. For example, we say "make a decision" – not "do a decision". We say "make a mistake" – not "do a mistake". (তবে "make" প্রতিটি শব্দের সাথে যায় না। উদাহরণস্বরূপ, আমরা "make a decision" বলি – "do a decision" নয়।)

📝 Think about it: "She made a mistake." Sounds natural, right?
📝 ভেবে দেখুন: "She made a mistake." স্বাভাবিক শোনায়, তাই না?
❌ "She did a mistake." Now it sounds wrong!
❌ "She did a mistake." এখন এটি ভুল শোনায়!

In this guide, I'll walk you through 50+ "make" collocations organised by meaning, with Bangla translations and example sentences. Let's get started! (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে ৫০+ "make" কলোকেশন শেখাবো, বাংলা অর্থ এবং উদাহরণ সহ। শুরু করা যাক!)

❌ Common Mistakes with "Make" Collocations

Many students make these common mistakes with "make". Let's look at them so you can avoid them. (অনেক শিক্ষার্থী "make" দিয়ে এই সাধারণ ভুলগুলি করে। আসুন সেগুলি দেখি যাতে আপনি সেগুলি এড়াতে পারেন।)

❌ "She does a mistake."
✅ "She makes a mistake."

📖 Explanation: "Make" is used with "mistake", not "do".

❌ "I need to do a decision."
✅ "I need to make a decision."

📖 Explanation: "Make" is used with "decision", not "do".

❌ "She made a favour for me."
✅ "She did me a favour."

📖 Explanation: "Favour" uses "do", not "make".

❌ "He made his homework."
✅ "He did his homework."

📖 Explanation: "Homework" uses "do", not "make".

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the meaning of "make collocations"? ▾
Make collocations are combinations of words where the verb "make" is used with a noun or phrase to create a common expression. For example, "make a decision" means to decide. These collocations are essential for speaking English naturally.
Why are "make" collocations important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
"Make" collocations are frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and vocabulary sections. Mastering them can help you score higher.
What is the difference between "make" and "do" collocations? ▾
"Make" collocations are for creating, producing, or causing something (make a decision, make a mistake). "Do" collocations are for activities, tasks, or duties (do homework, do business).
