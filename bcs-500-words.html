❌ Common BCS Vocabulary Mistakes

Many candidates lose marks due to these common mistakes. Avoid them to score higher in BCS.

❌ Mixing up "Affect" and "Effect"
✅ "Affect" = verb (to influence), "Effect" = noun (a result)

📖 Explanation: "The new policy will affect the economy." (verb) / "The effect was immediate." (noun)

❌ Confusing "Eminent" and "Imminent"
✅ "Eminent" = famous and respected, "Imminent" = about to happen

📖 Explanation: "He is an eminent scientist." / "The storm is imminent."

❌ Using "Less" instead of "Fewer"
✅ "Fewer" = countable items, "Less" = uncountable items

📖 Explanation: "Fewer books" (countable) / "Less water" (uncountable)

🎯 BCS Exam Preparation Strategy

📌 1. Daily Vocabulary Practice

Dedicate 15–20 minutes daily to learning new words. Use flashcards and spaced repetition for long-term retention. Apps like Ovidhan offer smart flashcards with SM-2 algorithm to help you remember words effectively.

📌 2. Practice with Past Papers

BCS exam questions often repeat. Practice with previous years' question papers to understand the pattern and identify recurring words.

📌 3. Learn Word Families

For each word, learn its noun, verb, adjective, and adverb forms. Example: success, succeed, successful, successfully.

📌 4. Focus on Commonly Tested Areas

  • Synonyms: Find the closest meaning of a given word.
  • Antonyms: Identify the opposite meaning.
  • One-word Substitutions: Replace a phrase with a single word.
  • Idioms and Phrases: Understand the meaning of common expressions.
  • Sentence Completion: Fill in blanks with appropriate vocabulary.

📌 5. Take Mock Tests

Practice with mock tests to simulate exam conditions. Regular testing helps identify weak areas and builds confidence.

🧠 Memory Tricks for BCS Vocabulary

  • Mnemonic: For "Abandon" – think of "a band on" – imagine a band playing and you leave (abandon) the concert.
  • Bangla Trick: Connect English words with similar-sounding Bangla words. Example: "Abhor" (ঘৃণা করা) – think of "আবার" – you don't want to see someone again.
  • Visualization: Picture the word in action. For "Accelerate", imagine a car speeding up.
  • Group by Theme: Learn words in groups – emotions, actions, places, time, etc.
  • Spaced Repetition: Review words regularly using flashcards. Learn 10 new words daily and review yesterday's words.
💡 Tip: Use the word in a sentence immediately after learning it. This connects the word to your real life.
