Introduction – Why Daily Sentences Matter

Speaking English fluently starts with building a habit of daily practice. For beginners, the best way to improve spoken English is to learn and repeat common daily sentences with Bangla translation.

In this article, you will find 100+ daily English speaking sentences categorized by real-life situations. Each sentence includes Bangla translation so you can understand and practice easily. Whether you are a student, job seeker, or preparing for IELTS, these sentences will help you speak English with more confidence.

💡 Tip: Practice 10 sentences daily – repeat each sentence 5 times aloud. Within one month, you will see significant improvement in your spoken English.

How to Practice Daily

Just reading these sentences is not enough. To really improve your spoken English, follow this daily practice routine:

  1. Learn 10 sentences daily – Focus on one category per day.
  2. Repeat each sentence 5 times aloud – Practice pronunciation and fluency.
  3. Record your voice – Listen back and identify areas for improvement.
  4. Practice with a partner – Ask and answer questions in English.
  5. Use the Ovidhan app – Practice vocabulary, pronunciation, and daily sentences offline.
📌 Speaking Tip: Speak English for 10–15 minutes daily without stopping. Don't worry about mistakes – focus on fluency first.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

As a beginner, you will make mistakes – and that's okay! Here are some common errors to avoid:

Mistake Correction Bangla Explanation
I am agree I agree Agree verb-এর সাথে am বসে না
She don't know She doesn't know Third person singular → doesn't
I have a work I have work Work uncountable noun
He go to office He goes to office Third person singular → goes
I am knowing I know Know-এর continuous tense হয় না
