📖 Introduction: What Are "Give" Collocations?

"Give" is one of the most common verbs in English. We use it for providing, offering, and transferring. ("Give" ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে সাধারণ ক্রিয়াপদগুলির মধ্যে একটি। আমরা এটি প্রদান, অফার এবং স্থানান্তরের জন্য ব্যবহার করি।)

However, "give" doesn't go with every word. For example, we say "give advice" – not "do advice". We say "give a gift" – not "make a gift". (তবে "give" প্রতিটি শব্দের সাথে যায় না। উদাহরণস্বরূপ, আমরা "give advice" বলি – "do advice" নয়।)

📝 "Can you give me some advice?" – Sounds natural, right?
📝 "আপনি কি আমাকে কিছু পরামর্শ দিতে পারেন?" – স্বাভাবিক শোনায়, তাই না?
❌ "Can you do me some advice?" – Not natural!
❌ "আপনি কি আমাকে কিছু পরামর্শ করতে পারেন?" – খুব স্বাভাবিক নয়!

In this guide, I'll walk you through 40+ "give" collocations organised by meaning, with Bangla translations and example sentences. Let's get started! (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে ৪০+ "give" কলোকেশন শেখাবো, বাংলা অর্থ এবং উদাহরণ সহ। শুরু করা যাক!)

⚡ Give vs Offer vs Provide – The Key Difference

This is one of the most common questions students ask. Here's the simple rule: (এটি শিক্ষার্থীদের সবচেয়ে সাধারণ প্রশ্নগুলির মধ্যে একটি। এখানে সহজ নিয়মটি রয়েছে:)

GIVE = transfer something to someone (general)
GIVE = কিছু স্থানান্তর করা (সাধারণ)
Examples: give advice, give a gift, give a hand
উদাহরণ: give advice, give a gift, give a hand

OFFER = give someone a choice (formal)
OFFER = কাউকে পছন্দ দেওয়া (আনুষ্ঠানিক)
Examples: offer a job, offer a drink, offer help
উদাহরণ: offer a job, offer a drink, offer help

PROVIDE = supply something needed (formal)
PROVIDE = প্রয়োজনীয় কিছু সরবরাহ করা (আনুষ্ঠানিক)
Examples: provide food, provide shelter, provide information
উদাহরণ: provide food, provide shelter, provide information
💡 Quick Memory Trick:
💡 দ্রুত মনে রাখার টিপ:
Give = general transfer (give advice, give a gift)
Give = সাধারণ স্থানান্তর (give advice, give a gift)
Offer = with a choice (offer a job, offer help)
Offer = পছন্দ সহ (offer a job, offer help)
Provide = supply something needed (provide food)
Provide = প্রয়োজনীয় কিছু সরবরাহ করা (provide food)

❌ Common Mistakes with "Give" Collocations

Many students make these common mistakes with "give". Let's look at them so you can avoid them. (অনেক শিক্ষার্থী "give" দিয়ে এই সাধারণ ভুলগুলি করে। আসুন সেগুলি দেখি যাতে আপনি সেগুলি এড়াতে পারেন।)

❌ "Can you do me some advice?"
✅ "Can you give me some advice?"

📖 Explanation: "Give" is used with "advice", not "do".

❌ "She made me a hand with the project."
✅ "She gave me a hand with the project."

📖 Explanation: "Give" is used with "a hand", not "make".

❌ "He did a speech at the conference."
✅ "He gave a speech at the conference."

📖 Explanation: "Give" is used with "a speech", not "do".

❌ "She took me permission to go."
✅ "She gave me permission to go."

📖 Explanation: "Give" is used with "permission", not "take".

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the meaning of "give collocations"? ▾
Give collocations are combinations of words where the verb "give" is used with a noun or phrase to create a common expression. For example, "give advice" means to offer advice. These collocations are essential for speaking English naturally.
Why are "give" collocations important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Give collocations are frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and vocabulary sections. Mastering them can help you score higher.
What is the difference between "give" and "offer"? ▾
Both "give" and "offer" mean to provide something. However, "give" is more common and casual. "Offer" is more formal and implies a choice. We say "give advice" more often than "offer advice", though both are correct.
Is it "give a hand" or "lend a hand"? ▾
Both are correct and mean the same thing – to help someone. "Give a hand" is more common in everyday conversation. "Lend a hand" is also used. Ovidhan includes both variations.
