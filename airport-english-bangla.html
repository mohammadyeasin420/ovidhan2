✈️ 1. Introduction – Why Airport English Matters

Let me tell you a story. A few years ago, I was at Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka, about to fly to London. I had my passport, my ticket, and my luggage. But when I got to the check-in counter, the agent asked me a question I didn't understand. My heart started racing. I panicked. I just pointed at my luggage and mumbled something. The agent looked confused. I felt so embarrassed. (কয়েক বছর আগে, আমি ঢাকার হযরত শাহজালাল আন্তর্জাতিক বিমানবন্দরে ছিলাম, লন্ডনের ফ্লাইট ধরার জন্য। আমার কাছে পাসপোর্ট, টিকিট এবং লাগেজ ছিল। কিন্তু চেক-ইন কাউন্টারে গিয়ে এজেন্ট আমাকে একটি প্রশ্ন করল যা আমি বুঝতে পারিনি। আমার হার্ট দ্রুত স্পন্দন শুরু করল। আমি আতঙ্কিত হয়ে পড়লাম। আমি শুধু আমার লাগেজের দিকে ইশারা করে কিছু বললাম। এজেন্ট বিভ্রান্ত দেখাল। আমি খুব বিব্রত বোধ করলাম।)

That experience taught me something important: airport English is not optional – it is essential. Whether you are traveling for work, study, or vacation, you will need to communicate at every step of the journey. The airport is a high-stress environment. There are announcements, signs, and people speaking quickly. If you don't understand, you can miss your flight, get stuck at immigration, or lose your luggage.

In this guide, I will teach you everything you need to know – from the moment you walk into the airport to the moment you walk out at your destination. I have included real conversations and Bangla translations for every sentence. I have also added Bangladesh-specific context – because traveling from Dhaka is different from traveling from London. By the end of this guide, you will feel confident navigating any airport, anywhere in the world. (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে বিমানবন্দরে প্রবেশ থেকে শুরু করে গন্তব্যে পৌঁছানো পর্যন্ত সবকিছু শেখাব।)

💡 A Personal Lesson

That embarrassing moment at the airport was a wake-up call for me. I realized that knowing grammar is not enough – I needed to know real-world English for real-world situations. That is why I created this guide. I don't want you to feel the same panic I felt.

🚗 2. Arriving at the Airport – Getting Started

These are the first phrases you will need when you arrive at the airport.

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি বিমানবন্দরে পৌঁছেছি।
📝 Literal: I have arrived at the airport.
🗣️ Natural: I'm at the airport.
💡 Simple and natural – you don't need to say "I have arrived." Just say "I'm at the airport."
🇧🇩 Bangla: চেক-ইন কাউন্টার কোথায়?
📝 Literal: Where is the check-in counter?
🗣️ Natural: Where is the check-in counter?
💡 This one is the same in literal and natural English. Simple and effective.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি এখান থেকে কীভাবে বিমানবন্দরে যাব?
📝 Literal: How do I go to the airport from here?
🗣️ Natural: How do I get to the airport from here?[reference:2]
💡 "Get to" is more natural than "go to" when asking for directions.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার ফ্লাইট কতক্ষণ আগে আসতে হবে?
📝 Literal: How long before my flight should I come?
🗣️ Natural: How early should I arrive for my flight?
💡 "How early" is the natural way to ask about recommended arrival time.
🇧🇩 Bangla: দয়া করে আমার ফ্লাইট ছাড়ার ৩ ঘণ্টা আগে বিমানবন্দরে আসবেন।
📝 Literal: Please come to the airport 3 hours before your flight departs.
🗣️ Natural: Please arrive at the airport 3 hours before your flight departs.[reference:3]
💡 "Arrive at" is more natural than "come to" in this context.
🧠 Pro Tip for Bangladeshi Travelers

For international flights from Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport, always arrive at least 3 hours early. For domestic flights, 1.5 to 2 hours is usually enough. This gives you time to check-in, clear security, and handle any unexpected delays.

🧳 3. Check-in & Luggage – Dropping Off Your Bags

Check-in is where you show your passport, get your boarding pass, and drop off your luggage. This is one of the most important steps. (চেক-ইন হলো যেখানে আপনি আপনার পাসপোর্ট দেখান, বোর্ডিং পাস পান এবং আপনার লাগেজ জমা দেন।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি আমার ফ্লাইটের জন্য চেক-ইন করতে চাই।
📝 Literal: I want to check-in for my flight.
🗣️ Natural: I'd like to check in for my flight, please. [reference:4]
💡 "I'd like" is more polite than "I want." Always say "please."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার পাসপোর্ট এবং টিকিট এখানে।
📝 Literal: My passport and ticket are here.
🗣️ Natural: Here is my passport and ticket.[reference:5]
💡 "Here is" is the natural way to hand something over. You can also say "Here you go."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কয়টি ব্যাগ চেক-ইন করতে পারি?
📝 Literal: How many bags can I check-in?
🗣️ Natural: How many bags am I allowed to check? [reference:6]
💡 "Allowed to check" is the standard phrase used by airline agents.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার লাগেজের ওজন কত?
📝 Literal: How much is the weight of my luggage?
🗣️ Natural: How much does my luggage weigh?
💡 "How much does... weigh?" is the correct question structure.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার লাগেজ ওভারওয়েট?
📝 Literal: Is my luggage overweight?
🗣️ Natural: Is my luggage overweight?
💡 This one is the same. Very useful if you've packed too much!
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি একটি জানালার আসন চাই।
📝 Literal: I want a window seat.
🗣️ Natural: I'd like a window seat, please. [reference:7]
💡 You can also ask for an "aisle seat" (পাশের আসন) if you prefer.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার বোর্ডিং পাস কোথায়?
📝 Literal: Where is my boarding pass?
🗣️ Natural: Here is your boarding pass.[reference:8]
💡 The agent will usually say this when handing it to you. Keep it safe!

🛂 4. Security Screening – Getting Through Safely

Security is where you put your bags on the conveyor belt and walk through the scanner. It is quick and routine, but you need to know the phrases. (সিকিউরিটি হলো যেখানে আপনি আপনার ব্যাগ কনভেয়র বেল্টে রাখেন এবং স্ক্যানারের মধ্য দিয়ে যান।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার ব্যাগ কনভেয়র বেল্টে রাখতে হবে?
📝 Literal: Do I put my bag on the conveyor belt?
🗣️ Natural: Should I put my bag on the conveyor belt?
💡 "Should I" is more natural and polite than "Do I."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার ল্যাপটপ বের করতে হবে?
📝 Literal: Do I need to take out my laptop?
🗣️ Natural: Do I need to remove my laptop from my bag?
💡 "Remove" is the standard word used by security officers.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার জুতা খুলতে হবে?
📝 Literal: Do I need to remove my shoes?
🗣️ Natural: Do I need to take off my shoes?
💡 "Take off" is more common than "remove" for shoes and clothes.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি আমার পকেট খালি করব।
📝 Literal: I will empty my pockets.
🗣️ Natural: I'll empty my pockets.
💡 "I'll" is a contraction of "I will" – more natural in spoken English.
🛂 Security Tips for Bangladeshi Travelers

At Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport, security screening is thorough. Make sure you remove your laptop and liquids from your bag. Liquids must be in containers of 100ml or less and placed in a clear plastic bag. If you are unsure, just ask: "Do I need to remove this?"

🛫 5. Boarding the Plane – Getting On

Boarding is when you actually get on the plane. Listen for announcements and follow the instructions. (বোর্ডিং হলো যখন আপনি আসলে প্লেনে উঠেন। ঘোষণা শুনুন এবং নির্দেশনা অনুসরণ করুন।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: গেট নম্বর কত?
📝 Literal: What is the gate number?
🗣️ Natural: What's the gate number? [reference:9]
💡 "What's" is a contraction of "What is" – more natural in conversation.
🇧🇩 Bangla: বোর্ডিং গেট কোথায়?
📝 Literal: Where is the boarding gate?
🗣️ Natural: Where is the boarding gate? [reference:10]
💡 This one is the same in literal and natural English. Very useful!
🇧🇩 Bangla: বোর্ডিং কখন শুরু হবে?
📝 Literal: When will boarding start?
🗣️ Natural: What time does boarding start? [reference:11]
💡 "What time" is more natural than "When" for asking about schedules.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি এখন বোর্ড করতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I board now?
🗣️ Natural: Am I ready to board?
💡 You don't need to ask – just wait for your group to be called.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার আসন নম্বর কোথায়?
📝 Literal: Where is my seat number?
🗣️ Natural: Where is my seat?
💡 Your seat number is on your boarding pass. Just say "Where is my seat?" if you need help.

🛩️ 6. In-Flight Communication – On the Plane

Once you are on the plane, you may need to communicate with the flight attendants or fellow passengers. (একবার আপনি প্লেনে উঠলে, আপনাকে ফ্লাইট অ্যাটেনডেন্ট বা সহযাত্রীদের সাথে কথা বলতে হতে পারে।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি এক গ্লাস পানি পেতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I get a glass of water?
🗣️ Natural: Could I have a glass of water, please? [reference:12]
💡 "Could I have" is more polite than "Can I get." Always add "please."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কি একটি কম্বল পেতে পারি?
📝 Literal: Can I get a blanket?
🗣️ Natural: Could I have a blanket, please? [reference:13]
💡 The same structure works for almost any in-flight request.
🇧🇩 Bangla: টয়লেট কোথায়?
📝 Literal: Where is the toilet?
🗣️ Natural: Where is the restroom?
💡 "Restroom" is more commonly used on planes. You can also say "bathroom."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার সিট বেল্ট বাঁধতে হবে?
📝 Literal: Do I need to fasten my seat belt?
🗣️ Natural: Should I fasten my seat belt?
💡 "Fasten" is the standard word for seat belts. You can also say "buckle up."
🇧🇩 Bangla: ফ্লাইটটি কতক্ষণ?
📝 Literal: How long is the flight?
🗣️ Natural: How long is the flight?
💡 This one is the same. You can also ask "What's the flight duration?"

🛬 7. Arrival & Immigration – Entering a New Country

When you arrive at your destination, you will go through immigration. The officer will ask you questions. Here is what to expect. (আপনি যখন আপনার গন্তব্যে পৌঁছান, আপনি ইমিগ্রেশনের মধ্য দিয়ে যাবেন। অফিসার আপনাকে প্রশ্ন করবে।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনার ভ্রমণের উদ্দেশ্য কী? [reference:14]
📝 Literal: What is the purpose of your visit?
🗣️ Natural: What is the purpose of your visit? [reference:15]
💡 This is a common immigration question. Answer honestly: "Tourism," "Business," or "Study."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনি কতদিন থাকবেন? [reference:16]
📝 Literal: How long will you stay?
🗣️ Natural: How long will you be staying? [reference:17]
💡 "Be staying" is slightly more natural than "stay."
🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনি কোথায় থাকবেন? [reference:18]
📝 Literal: Where will you stay?
🗣️ Natural: Where are you staying? [reference:19]
💡 "Are you staying" (present continuous) is more natural for immediate plans.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আপনার কাছে কি রিটার্ন টিকিট আছে? [reference:20]
📝 Literal: Do you have a return ticket?
🗣️ Natural: Do you have a return ticket? [reference:21]
💡 This one is the same. Always carry proof of your return or onward travel.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি ব্যবসায়িক সফরে এসেছি।
📝 Literal: I have come on a business trip.
🗣️ Natural: I'm here on business.
💡 "I'm here on..." is a very natural way to state your purpose.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি পর্যটন করতে এসেছি।
📝 Literal: I have come for tourism.
🗣️ Natural: I'm here on holiday. / I'm here for tourism.
💡 "On holiday" is more common in British English. "On vacation" is American.
🛂 Immigration Tips for Bangladeshi Travelers

At immigration, always answer honestly and concisely. Do not give long explanations – the officer just needs basic facts. Keep your passport, visa, and return ticket ready. If you are transiting through another country, say "I am in transit" or "I have a connecting flight."

🧾 8. Customs & Baggage – Declaring Your Items

After immigration, you collect your luggage and go through customs. Here is what you need to know. (ইমিগ্রেশনের পর, আপনি আপনার লাগেজ সংগ্রহ করেন এবং কাস্টমসের মধ্য দিয়ে যান।)

🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার লাগেজ কোথায় পাব?
📝 Literal: Where will I get my luggage?
🗣️ Natural: Where can I collect my luggage?
💡 "Collect" is the standard word for picking up luggage at baggage claim.
🇧🇩 Bangla: কাস্টমস কোথায়?
📝 Literal: Where is customs?
🗣️ Natural: Where is customs? [reference:22]
💡 "Customs" is used as a singular noun in this context.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমার কিছু ঘোষণা করতে হবে?
📝 Literal: Do I need to declare anything?
🗣️ Natural: Do I have anything to declare? [reference:23]
💡 "Do I have anything to declare?" is the standard customs question.
🇧🇩 Bangla: আমি কোনো নিষিদ্ধ জিনিস আনিনি।
📝 Literal: I have not brought any prohibited items.
🗣️ Natural: I don't have anything to declare.
💡 This is the standard answer if you have nothing to declare.
🇧🇩 Customs Tips for Returning Bangladeshis

At Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport, there are two channels at customs: the Green Channel (for passengers with nothing to declare) and the Red Channel (for passengers with dutiable goods)[reference:24]. If you are bringing in more than USD 10,000 or equivalent in foreign currency, you must declare it and fill out the FMJ form[reference:25]. When in doubt, use the Red Channel.

💬 10. Real Dialogues – Airport Conversations

Here are three real airport conversations. Read them aloud and practice with a friend. (এখানে তিনটি বাস্তব বিমানবন্দর কথোপকথন দেওয়া হলো। সেগুলি জোরে পড়ুন এবং একজন বন্ধুর সাথে অনুশীলন করুন।)

🟡 Dialogue 1: Check-in at the Counter

Agent: Good morning. Where are you flying to today?
সুপ্রভাত। আপনি আজ কোথায় যাচ্ছেন?

You: I'm flying to London.
আমি লন্ডন যাচ্ছি।

Agent: May I have your passport and ticket, please? [reference:38]
আপনার পাসপোর্ট এবং টিকিট দিতে পারবেন?

You: Here you are.
এই নিন।

Agent: How many bags are you checking in? [reference:39]
আপনি কয়টি ব্যাগ চেক-ইন করবেন?

You: One bag, please.
একটি ব্যাগ, দয়া করে।

Agent: Would you like a window or aisle seat? [reference:40]
আপনি জানালার আসন চান নাকি পাশের আসন?

You: A window seat, please.
জানালার আসন, দয়া করে।

Agent: Here is your boarding pass. Your flight departs from Gate 26 at 9:30 AM.[reference:41]
আপনার বোর্ডিং পাস। আপনার ফ্লাইট সকাল ৯:৩০ এ গেট ২৬ থেকে ছাড়বে।

You: Thank you very much.
আপনাকে অনেক ধন্যবাদ।

🟢 Dialogue 2: Immigration at Arrival

Officer: Welcome. What is the purpose of your visit? [reference:42]
স্বাগতম। আপনার ভ্রমণের উদ্দেশ্য কী?

আমি পর্যটনে এসেছি।

আপনি কতদিন থাকবেন?

দুই সপ্তাহ।

আপনি কোথায় থাকবেন?

You: At a hotel in the city center.
শহরের কেন্দ্রস্থলে একটি হোটেলে।

Officer: Do you have a return ticket? [reference:45]
আপনার কাছে কি রিটার্ন টিকিট আছে?

You: Yes, I do. Here it is.
হ্যাঁ, আছে। এই নিন।

Officer: Enjoy your stay. [reference:46]
আপনার অবস্থান উপভোগ করুন।

অবশ্যই। আমি তা নিয়ে আসছি।

🌍 11. Cultural Tips – Airport Etiquette Around the World

Knowing the language is important, but knowing the culture is just as important. Here are some cultural tips for air travel. (ভাষা জানা গুরুত্বপূর্ণ, কিন্তু সংস্কৃতি জানাও সমান গুরুত্বপূর্ণ। এখানে বিমান ভ্রমণের জন্য কিছু সাংস্কৃতিক টিপস দেওয়া হলো।)

🕐 Arrive Early
For international flights, arrive at least 3 hours early[reference:49]. For domestic flights, 1.5 to 2 hours is enough. This gives you time for check-in, security, and unexpected delays.
🙋 Be Polite
Always say "please," "thank you," and "excuse me" to airport staff. They deal with hundreds of passengers every day, and politeness goes a long way.
🧳 Luggage Porters
In some countries, it is customary to tip porters who help with your luggage. In Bangladesh, a small tip (50-100 BDT) is appreciated but not required.
📢 Listen to Announcements
Airport announcements can be hard to understand. If you miss something, go to the information desk and ask: "Excuse me, what was the announcement for Flight [number]?"

⚠️ 12. Common Mistakes – Airport English

Avoid these common errors that come from direct Bangla translation.

❌ Wrong (Literal) ✅ Correct (Natural) 💡 Why?
I want to check-in. I'd like to check in, please.[reference:50] "I'd like" is more polite than "I want."
Where is my luggage? Where can I collect my luggage? "Collect" is the standard word for picking up luggage.
I am wanting a window seat. I'd like a window seat, please.[reference:51] "Wanting" is not used in this way. Use "I would like."
How much time to boarding? What time does boarding start? [reference:52] "What time" is the natural way to ask about schedules.
I go to Dhaka. I'm flying to Dhaka. "Flying to" is specific to air travel. "Going to" is too general.

❓ 14. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check-in at the airport in English? ▾
Say "I'd like to check in for my flight, please." Then hand over your passport and ticket.[reference:62][reference:63] The agent will ask "How many bags are you checking in?"[reference:64] and "Would you like a window or aisle seat?"[reference:65] Answer honestly and politely.
What questions does immigration ask in English? ▾
Immigration officers often ask: "What is the purpose of your visit?"[reference:66] "How long will you stay?"[reference:67] "Where are you staying?"[reference:68] and "Do you have a return ticket?"[reference:69] Answer concisely and honestly.
How do I ask for help at the airport in English? ▾
Say "Excuse me, could you help me?" or "Can you tell me where the boarding gate is?"[reference:70] Always start with "Excuse me" to get someone's attention politely.
What is the difference between "check-in" and "boarding"? ▾
Check-in is when you arrive at the airport, show your passport, and drop off your luggage. Boarding is when you actually get on the plane. Boarding usually starts 30-45 minutes before departure[reference:71].
How do I say "I am in transit" in English? ▾
You say "I am in transit"[reference:72] or "I have a connecting flight." If you are just passing through, say "I am transiting through [city] on my way to [destination]."
