📖 1. Introduction

Future Simple Tense (সাধারণ ভবিষ্যৎ কাল) is used to describe actions that will happen in the future. It is the most basic way to talk about future events. (Future Simple Tense ভবিষ্যতে ঘটবে এমন কাজ বোঝাতে ব্যবহৃত হয়। এটি ভবিষ্যতের কাজ সম্পর্কে কথা বলার সবচেয়ে মৌলিক উপায়।)

For Bangladeshi students, Future Simple is usually the first future tense they learn. The main challenge is knowing when to use 'will' vs 'going to'. This guide will clear up all confusion. (বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীদের জন্য Future Simple সাধারণত প্রথম ভবিষ্যৎ কাল যা তারা শেখে। মূল চ্যালেঞ্জ হল 'will' এবং 'going to'-এর মধ্যে পার্থক্য বোঝা। এই গাইড সব বিভ্রান্তি দূর করবে।)

📚 2. Definition & Usage

Future Simple Tense is used to describe:

  • Predictions about the future.
    (ভবিষ্যতের পূর্বাভাস।)
    Example: "It will rain tomorrow."
  • Spontaneous decisions (made at the moment of speaking).
    (কথা বলার সময় নেওয়া সিদ্ধান্ত।)
    Example: "I'm hungry. I will eat something."
  • Promises and offers.
    (প্রতিশ্রুতি এবং প্রস্তাব।)
    Example: "I will help you with your homework."
  • Warnings and threats.
    (সতর্কতা এবং হুমকি।)
    Example: "You will be late if you don't hurry."
  • Facts about the future.
    (ভবিষ্যতের সত্য ঘটনা।)
    Example: "The sun will rise at 6 AM tomorrow."
👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Students remember this easily: Will = predictions, promises, spontaneous decisions. Going to = plans and evidence-based predictions.

📐 3. Structure – Affirmative, Negative & Interrogative

🔹 Affirmative (হ্যাঁ বোধক)

Subject + will/shall + Verb (base form)
SubjectModalV1Example
IwillgoI will go to Dhaka.
YouwillreadYou will read the book.
HewilleatHe will eat rice.
ShewillwriteShe will write a letter.
ItwillrainIt will rain tomorrow.
WewillplayWe will play cricket.
TheywillfinishThey will finish their work.

Note: 'Shall' can be used with I and We, but it is formal and less common. 'Will' is used with all subjects in modern English.

🔸 Negative (না বোধক)

Subject + will/shall + not + Verb (base form)
SubjectModalNotV1Example
IwillnotgoI will not go to Dhaka.
HewillnoteatHe will not eat rice.
TheywillnotfinishThey will not finish their work.

Short forms: won't = will not, shan't = shall not

🔹 Interrogative (প্রশ্ন বোধক)

Will/Shall + Subject + Verb (base form)?
ModalSubjectV1Example
WillyougoWill you go to Dhaka?
WillheeatWill he eat rice?
WilltheyfinishWill they finish their work?

⚡ 5. Mini Comparison – Future Simple vs 'Going to'

This is a common confusion. Here's the clear difference: (এটি একটি সাধারণ বিভ্রান্তি। এখানে স্পষ্ট পার্থক্য রয়েছে:)

FeatureFuture Simple (will)'Going to'
Usage Predictions, promises, spontaneous decisions Plans, intentions, evidence-based predictions
Structure Subject + will + V1 Subject + am/is/are + going to + V1
Example I will help you. I am going to study.
Bangla আমি তোমাকে সাহায্য করব। আমি পড়াশোনা করতে যাচ্ছি।
💡 Quick Memory Trick:
Spontaneous → Will  |  Planned → Going to
হঠাৎ সিদ্ধান্ত → Will  |  পরিকল্পিত → Going to

Rule 1: Predictions

✅ "It will rain tomorrow."
✅ "আগামীকাল বৃষ্টি হবে।"
✅ "He will be a doctor."
✅ "সে ডাক্তার হবে।"

Rule 2: Spontaneous Decisions

✅ "I'm thirsty. I will drink some water."
✅ "আমার তেষ্টা পেয়েছে। আমি পানি পান করব।"
✅ "This bag is heavy. I will carry it for you."
✅ "এই ব্যাগটি ভারী। আমি এটি আপনার জন্য বহন করব।"

Rule 3: Promises

✅ "I promise I will help you."
✅ "আমি প্রতিশ্রুতি দিচ্ছি, আমি আপনাকে সাহায্য করব।"
✅ "I will always remember you."
✅ "আমি আপনাকে সবসময় মনে রাখব।"

Rule 4: Offers

✅ "I will make some tea for you."
✅ "আমি আপনার জন্য চা বানাব।"
✅ "We will help you with the project."
✅ "আমরা আপনাকে প্রজেক্টে সাহায্য করব।"

Rule 5: Warnings and Threats

✅ "You will be late if you don't hurry."
✅ "তাড়াতাড়ি না করলে আপনি দেরি করবেন।"

✅ "ফোন না করলে সে রাগ করবে।"

❌ 7. Common Mistakes – Bangladeshi Students

❌ "I will went to Dhaka tomorrow."
✅ "I will go to Dhaka tomorrow."

📖 After "will", always use the base form (V1) – "go", not "went".

❌ "He will not goes to school."
✅ "He will not go to school."

📖 After "will not", use the base form (V1) – "go", not "goes".

❌ "Will he goes to school?"
✅ "Will he go to school?"

📖 After "Will", use the base form (V1) – "go", not "goes".

❌ "I will to go to Dhaka."
✅ "I will go to Dhaka."

📖 Don't use "to" after "will". Simply use the base form.

🚫 11. When NOT to Use Future Simple

Future Simple is not used for: (Future Simple ব্যবহার করা হয় না:)

  • Plans and intentions – Use 'going to' instead.
  • Actions already decided – Use 'going to' or Present Continuous.
  • Predictions based on evidence – Use 'going to' (e.g., "Look at those clouds. It's going to rain.")
❌ "I will visit my grandmother tomorrow." (if it's a plan)
✅ "I am going to visit my grandmother tomorrow."

📖 If it's a pre-planned action, use "going to" or Present Continuous for arrangements.

❌ "Look at those dark clouds. It will rain."
✅ "Look at those dark clouds. It is going to rain."

📖 Evidence-based predictions → use "going to".

👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Remember: Use will for spontaneous decisions, promises, and general predictions. Use going to for plans and evidence-based predictions.

👨‍🏫 13. Teacher Tips

💡 Tip 1 – Will vs Going to

Spontaneous decisions = will (e.g., "I'll help you."). Plans = going to (e.g., "I'm going to study."). Practice with scenarios!

💡 Tip 2 – Signal Words

Tomorrow, next week, soon – these words are your clues for Future Simple. If you see them, use "will".

💡 Tip 3 – Common Exam Trap

In exams, they often test: will + V1 (not V2). Remember: "will go" not "will went". Also, "shall" with I/We is considered formal but still tested.

🎯 14. BCS & Bank Job Section

📘 Previous BCS Patterns

  • Error Correction: Identify and correct "will" + V2 errors (should be V1).
  • Sentence Completion: Fill in the blank with the correct future form.
  • Transformation: Change sentences from "going to" to "will" and vice versa.
Example 1 (Error Correction):
❌ "He will went to Dhaka tomorrow."
✅ "He will go to Dhaka tomorrow."
Example 2 (Sentence Completion):
"I ______ (help) you with your homework."
✅ "I will help you with your homework."
Example 3 (Transformation):
"I am going to study." (Change to will)
✅ "I will study."

💡 Tips for BCS

  • Remember: will + V1 (base form).
  • Look for signal words: tomorrow, next week, soon
  • 'Shall' with I/We is sometimes tested, but 'will' is more common.

❓ 19. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Future Simple Tense in Bangla? ▾
Future Simple Tense (সাধারণ ভবিষ্যৎ কাল) is used to describe actions that will happen in the future. For example: 'I will go to Dhaka' – আমি ঢাকায় যাব।
What is the structure of Future Simple Tense? ▾
The structure is: Subject + will/shall + Verb (base form). For example: I will go, He will eat, They will play. Negative: Subject + will/shall + not + V1. Interrogative: Will/Shall + Subject + V1?
What is the difference between 'will' and 'shall'? ▾
In modern English, 'will' is used with all subjects. 'Shall' is traditionally used with I and We, but it is now considered formal and less common. For exams, both are sometimes tested, but 'will' is more common.
What is the difference between Future Simple and 'going to'? ▾
Future Simple ('will') is used for spontaneous decisions, predictions, and promises. 'Going to' is used for planned actions and predictions based on evidence. For example: 'I will help you' (spontaneous) vs 'I am going to study' (plan).
How do we form negative sentences in Future Simple? ▾
We use 'will not' (won't) or 'shall not' (shan't) + the base form of the verb. For example: 'I will not go' or 'I won't go.'
What are the common signal words for Future Simple? ▾
Common signal words are: tomorrow, next week, next month, next year, in the future, soon, later, this evening, tonight.
Why is Future Simple important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Future Simple is frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and transformation. The use of 'will' vs 'shall' and the base form are especially important.

📝 20. Summary

Future Simple Tense is used to describe future actions. Here are the key takeaways:

  • Structure: Subject + will/shall + V1
  • Negative: Subject + will/shall + not + V1
  • Interrogative: Will/Shall + Subject + V1?
  • Usage: Predictions, promises, offers, spontaneous decisions, warnings
  • Signal Words: tomorrow, next week, soon, later
  • Avoid: Using V2 after "will" (e.g., "will went" is wrong)
  • Key Difference: Will = spontaneous/prediction | Going to = plan/evidence
💡 Golden Rule

"If it's a decision made now or a prediction, use will. If it's a plan or evidence, use going to."

"যদি এটি এখন নেওয়া সিদ্ধান্ত বা পূর্বাভাস হয়, will ব্যবহার করুন। যদি এটি পরিকল্পনা বা প্রমাণ হয়, going to ব্যবহার করুন।"
