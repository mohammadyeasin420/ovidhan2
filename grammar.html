📌 What is English Grammar? (ইংরেজি ব্যাকরণ কী?)

English Grammar is the set of rules that tells us how to combine words to form correct and meaningful sentences. Just like Bengali has grammar rules, English also has its own rules that we must follow to speak and write correctly.

Bangla (বাংলা): ইংরেজি ব্যাকরণ হলো সেই নিয়মগুলোর সমষ্টি যা আমাদের শেখায় কীভাবে শব্দগুলোর মাধ্যমে সঠিক ও অর্থপূর্ণ বাক্য তৈরি করতে হয়। বাংলা ভাষার যেমন ব্যাকরণ আছে, ইংরেজিরও তেমনই নিজস্ব নিয়ম আছে যা আমাদের মেনে চলতে হয়।

📖 Grammar is the foundation of any language.
Without grammar, your sentences become unclear. With good grammar, you can express your thoughts clearly and confidently.

Why is grammar important?

  • Speaking: Speak clearly and be understood
  • Writing: Write correctly for exams, emails, and applications
  • Reading: Understand English texts better
  • Listening: Follow conversations and lectures
  • Exams: Score higher in BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs, University Admission
  • Career: Communicate professionally in interviews and workplace

📝 Sentence Structure (বাক্য গঠন)

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. Every sentence has a Subject and a Verb.

🔑 Basic Structure:
Subject + Verb + Object = Complete Sentence

Example: I + read + a book.
He + eats + rice.
She + writes + a letter.

Key elements of a sentence:

  • Subject (কর্তা): Who or what does the action (I, He, The boy)
  • Verb (ক্রিয়া): The action or state (read, eats, is)
  • Object (কর্ম): Who or what receives the action (a book, rice)
  • Complement (পূরক): Completes the meaning (beautiful, tall)
  • Modifier (বিশেষক): Adds description (quickly, very)
💡 Pro Tip: Every English sentence must have at least a subject and a verb. Without these, it's not a complete sentence.

📝 Subject-Verb Agreement (কর্তা-ক্রিয়ার মিল)

Subject-Verb Agreement means that the verb must match the subject in number (singular/plural) and person.

🔑 Golden Rule:
Singular Subject → Singular Verb
Plural Subject → Plural Verb

He goes to school. (Singular)
They go to school. (Plural)

Most Important Rules:

  • Everyone, Someone, Nobody → Singular Verb (Everyone is present)
  • One of + Plural Noun → Singular Verb (One of the students is absent)
  • A number of → Plural Verb (A number of students are present)
  • The number of → Singular Verb (The number of students is fifty)
  • Neither...nor / Either...or → Verb agrees with the closest subject
  • Collective Nouns → Singular Verb (The team is winning)

⏳ Tenses (কাল)

There are 12 tenses in English. They show when an action happens — present, past, or future.

Time Indefinite Continuous Perfect Perfect Continuous
Present I read I am reading I have read I have been reading
Past I read (V2) I was reading I had read I had been reading
Future I will read I will be reading I will have read I will have been reading

Quick Tips:

  • Present Indefinite: Habits and universal truths (He goes to school)
  • Present Continuous: Actions happening now (He is going to school)
  • Present Perfect: Past actions with present results (He has gone to school)
  • Past Indefinite: Completed past actions (He went to school)
  • Future Indefinite: Future actions (He will go to school)

📋 Articles (a, an, the)

Articles are special words that come before nouns. There are three articles in English: a, an, the.

🔑 Quick Rules:
A → Consonant sound: a book, a university
An → Vowel sound: an apple, an hour
The → Specific or unique: the sun, the book I read

Tricky Cases:

  • a university (U sounds like 'Y' → consonant sound)
  • an honest man (H silent → vowel sound)
  • an MBA (M sounds like 'Em' → vowel sound)
  • a European (E sounds like 'Y' → consonant sound)
  • the United States (Plural country name → 'the')
  • Mount Everest (Single mountain → no article)

🔄 Pronouns (সর্বনাম)

Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition. There are 8 types of pronouns.

  • Personal: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
  • Possessive: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
  • Reflexive: myself, yourself, himself, herself
  • Demonstrative: this, that, these, those
  • Indefinite: everyone, someone, nobody, all
  • Interrogative: who, whom, whose, which, what
  • Relative: who, whom, which, that, whose
  • Reciprocal: each other, one another

🎨 Adjectives (বিশেষণ)

Adjectives describe nouns or pronouns. There are 8 types of adjectives.

  • Descriptive: beautiful, tall, honest
  • Demonstrative: this, that, these, those
  • Possessive: my, your, his, her, our, their
  • Interrogative: which, what, whose
  • Indefinite: some, any, many, few
  • Distributive: each, every, either, neither
  • Quantitative: one, two, much, little
  • Proper: Bangladeshi, Chinese, Shakespearean

Degree of Comparison: Positive (tall) → Comparative (taller) → Superlative (tallest)

⏩ Adverbs (ক্রিয়া বিশেষণ)

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. There are 8 types.

  • Manner: quickly, slowly, carefully
  • Place: here, there, everywhere
  • Time: now, then, yesterday
  • Frequency: always, never, sometimes
  • Degree: very, extremely, quite
  • Reason: therefore, hence, consequently
  • Affirmation/Negation: yes, no, certainly
  • Interrogative: why, where, when, how

Confusing Pairs: hard/hardly, late/lately, near/nearly

📍 Prepositions (পদান্বয়ী অব্যয়)

Prepositions show the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word. There are 3 types.

  • Time: in, on, at, since, for, during
  • Place: in, on, at, under, over, between, among
  • Movement: to, from, into, out of, across, through

Common Collocations: afraid of, good at, interested in, senior to, married to

🔗 Conjunctions (সংযোজক)

Conjunctions join words, phrases, or clauses. There are 3 types.

  • Coordinating (FANBOYS): For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So
  • Subordinating: because, although, since, while, if, unless, when
  • Correlative: either...or, neither...nor, both...and, not only...but also

Important: Because and So (don't use together), Although and But (don't use together)

❗ Interjections (আবেগসূচক শব্দ)

Interjections express sudden emotions or feelings.

  • Joy: Hurrah!, Hooray!, Yay!
  • Sorrow: Alas!, Oh dear!
  • Surprise: Wow!, Oh!, What!
  • Approval: Bravo!, Well done!, Great!
  • Disapproval: Ugh!, Oh no!
  • Pain: Ouch!, Ow!
  • Greeting: Hello!, Hi!, Hey!
  • Other: Well, Hmm, Oh well, Oops

Punctuation: Strong emotion → (!), Mild emotion → (,)

📌 Determiners (নির্ধারক)

Determiners come before nouns to show which one or how many.

  • Articles: a, an, the
  • Demonstratives: this, that, these, those
  • Possessives: my, your, his, her, our, their
  • Quantifiers: some, any, many, much, few, little, all, both, every
  • Numbers: one, two, three, first, second, third

Difference: Few = almost none (negative), A few = some (positive). Little = almost none (negative), A little = some (positive).

⚡ Modals (সাহায্যকারী ক্রিয়া)

Modal verbs help the main verb express possibility, necessity, permission, or ability.

  • Can: Ability (I can swim)
  • Could: Past ability / Polite request (Could you help me?)
  • May: Permission (May I come in?)
  • Might: Possibility (It might rain)
  • Must: Necessity (You must study)
  • Should: Advice (You should exercise)
  • Would: Polite request / Habit (I would like tea)
  • Need: Necessity (You need to go)
  • Ought to: Moral duty (You ought to help)

Rule: Modals are always followed by the base form (V1) of the verb. (I can go, not I can going).

🔄 Voice Change (বাচ্য পরিবর্তন)

Voice shows whether the subject does the action or receives the action.

🔑 Two Types:
Active Voice: Subject does the action (He writes a letter)
Passive Voice: Subject receives the action (A letter is written by him)

Structure:

  • Active: Subject + Verb + Object
  • Passive: Object + Auxiliary Verb + V3 + by + Subject

Examples:

  • Active: He writes a letter → Passive: A letter is written by him
  • Active: She is reading a book → Passive: A book is being read by her
  • Active: They have finished the work → Passive: The work has been finished by them

💬 Narration (বক্তৃতা পরিবর্তন)

Narration is reporting what someone said. There are two types.

🔑 Two Types:
Direct Speech: Exact words (He said, "I am happy")
Indirect Speech: Reported words (He said that he was happy)

Rules:

  • Tense Changes: Present → Past, Past → Past Perfect
  • Pronoun Changes: I → He/She, We → They, You → He/She/They
  • Reporting Verbs: said, told, asked, requested
  • Time Changes: now → then, today → that day, yesterday → the previous day

Examples:

  • Direct: He said, "I am happy." → Indirect: He said that he was happy.
  • Direct: She said, "I will come." → Indirect: She said that she would come.
  • Direct: He said, "I have finished." → Indirect: He said that he had finished.

🔄 Transformation (বাক্য রূপান্তর)

Transformation means changing a sentence from one form to another without changing the meaning.

Types of Transformation:

  • Affirmative ↔ Negative: He is honest → He is not dishonest
  • Assertive ↔ Interrogative: He is a student → Isn't he a student?
  • Assertive ↔ Exclamatory: It is a beautiful day → What a beautiful day!
  • Simple ↔ Complex ↔ Compound: Simple: He is too tired to walk → Complex: He is so tired that he cannot walk → Compound: He is very tired and cannot walk
  • Degree Change: Positive: No other boy is as tall as Rahim → Comparative: Rahim is taller than any other boy → Superlative: Rahim is the tallest boy
  • Voice Change: Active → Passive
  • Narration: Direct → Indirect

✅ Right Form of Verb (ক্রিয়ার সঠিক রূপ)

Choosing the correct form of the verb based on tense, subject, and context.

Most Important Rules:

  • Present Indefinite: He goes to school. (Habit)
  • Present Continuous: He is going to school. (Now)
  • Present Perfect: He has gone to school. (Just finished)
  • Past Indefinite: He went to school. (Yesterday)
  • Future Indefinite: He will go to school. (Tomorrow)
  • Modals: He can go to school. (Modal + V1)
  • Prepositions: He is good at writing. (Preposition + Ving)
  • To: He wants to go. (To + V1)

⚠️ Common Grammar Mistakes (সাধারণ ভুল)

❌ Common Mistake ✅ Correct Form Why?
He go to school. He goes to school. Singular subject → verb + s/es
I am agree. I agree. Agree is a verb — no 'am' needed
Everyone are happy. Everyone is happy. Everyone is singular
He is a honest man. He is an honest man. Honest has a vowel sound → 'an'
I have a work. I have work. Work is uncountable
She is senior than me. She is senior to me. Senior takes 'to', not 'than'
He is married with a doctor. He is married to a doctor. Married to (not with)
I have been here for 2015. I have been here since 2015. Since + point in time, For + duration
The Mount Everest is high. Mount Everest is high. No article before single mountains
She sings beautiful. She sings beautifully. Action verb → adverb

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is English grammar?
English grammar is the set of rules that governs how words are combined to form sentences. It includes rules for parts of speech, tense, voice, narration, sentence structure, and more.
How long does it take to learn English grammar?
With consistent daily practice, you can learn the basics in 2-3 months. To master advanced grammar concepts, it may take 6-12 months of regular study and practice.
Which grammar topic should beginners learn first?
Beginners should start with Parts of Speech, as it forms the foundation of all grammar. Then move to Sentence Structure, Subject-Verb Agreement, and Tenses.
Is grammar necessary for speaking English?
Yes, grammar is essential for speaking English clearly and correctly. It helps you form proper sentences and communicate your ideas effectively.
Which grammar topics are important for BCS exams?
The most important topics are Subject-Verb Agreement, Tense, Voice Change, Narration, Articles, Prepositions, and Sentence Correction. These topics frequently appear in BCS Preliminary and Written exams.
Which grammar topics are important for IELTS?
For IELTS Writing and Speaking, focus on Tenses, Subject-Verb Agreement, Sentence Structure, and Using a Range of Grammar Structures. For Reading and Listening, understanding sentence structures is important.
Can I learn grammar without a teacher?
Yes, you can learn grammar independently using quality resources. This Grammar Hub provides all the lessons you need, and the Ovidhan app offers practice exercises and quizzes.
How should I practice grammar daily?
Read one grammar lesson daily, solve 10-15 practice questions, take a quiz, and review your mistakes. Using the Ovidhan app's grammar exercises and tools will help you practice consistently.
What are the most common grammar mistakes?
The most common mistakes include: Subject-Verb Agreement errors, wrong tense usage, incorrect article usage, misplaced modifiers, and confusing adverb/adjective usage.
What is the difference between active and passive voice?
Active voice focuses on the subject doing the action (He writes a letter). Passive voice focuses on the action itself (A letter is written by him). Active voice is more common in everyday English.
What is the difference between direct and indirect speech?
Direct speech quotes the exact words spoken (He said, 'I am happy'). Indirect speech reports what was said (He said that he was happy).
What are the 8 parts of speech?
The 8 parts of speech are: Noun, Pronoun, Verb, Adjective, Adverb, Preposition, Conjunction, and Interjection. Each plays a different role in a sentence.
How many tenses are there in English?
There are 12 tenses in English: Present (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous), Past (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous), and Future (Indefinite, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous).
What is Subject-Verb Agreement?
Subject-Verb Agreement means that the verb must match the subject in number (singular or plural) and person. If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular; if plural, the verb must be plural.
How can I improve my grammar for competitive exams?
