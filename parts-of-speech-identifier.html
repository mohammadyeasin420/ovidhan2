📚 What Are the Parts of Speech?

Every word in English belongs to one of eight categories called parts of speech. Understanding these categories is essential for BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs, HSC, and university admission. (ইংরেজির প্রতিটি শব্দ আটটি শ্রেণির একটিতে পড়ে, যাকে Parts of Speech বলে।)

1. Noun (বিশেষ্য)

A noun names a person, place, thing, or idea.

📝 Rahim, Dhaka, book, happiness
রহিম, ঢাকা, বই, সুখ

📖 Learn more about Noun Rules →

2. Pronoun (সর্বনাম)

A pronoun replaces a noun to avoid repetition.

📝 He, she, it, they, we, I
সে, তিনি, এটা, তারা, আমরা, আমি

📖 Learn more about Pronoun Rules →

3. Verb (ক্রিয়া)

A verb describes an action, occurrence, or state of being.

📝 run, eat, is, become, have
দৌড়ানো, খাওয়া, হওয়া, হয়ে যাওয়া, থাকা

📖 Learn more about Verb Rules →

4. Adjective (বিশেষণ)

An adjective describes or modifies a noun or pronoun.

📝 beautiful, tall, happy, red
সুন্দর, লম্বা, সুখী, লাল

📖 Learn more about Adjective Rules →

5. Adverb (ক্রিয়াবিশেষণ)

An adverb modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

📝 quickly, very, always, well
দ্রুত, খুব, সবসময়, ভালো

📖 Learn more about Adverb Rules →

6. Preposition (পদান্বয়ী অব্যয়)

A preposition shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word.

📝 in, on, at, to, for, with, by
মধ্যে, উপরে, এ, প্রতি, জন্য, সাথে, দ্বারা

📖 Learn more about Preposition Rules →

7. Conjunction (সংযোজক অব্যয়)

A conjunction connects words, phrases, or clauses.

📝 and, but, or, because, although
এবং, কিন্তু, অথবা, কারণ, যদিও

📖 Learn more about Conjunction Rules →

8. Interjection (আবেগসূচক অব্যয়)

An interjection expresses strong emotion.

📝 Wow! Oh! Ouch! Hurrah!
বাহ! ওহ! আউচ! হুররে!

📖 Learn more about Interjection Rules →

❌ Common Mistakes

❌ "She is a beauty girl."
✅ "She is a beautiful girl."

📖 Explanation: "Beauty" is a noun. "Beautiful" is the adjective that describes the girl.

❌ "He runs quick."
✅ "He runs quickly."

📖 Explanation: "Quick" is an adjective. "Quickly" is the adverb that modifies the verb "runs".

🧠 Quick Tip

💡 Remember: When analyzing a sentence, ask yourself:
• Is it a person, place, thing, or idea? → Noun
• Does it replace a noun? → Pronoun
• Does it describe a noun? → Adjective
• Does it describe a verb, adjective, or adverb? → Adverb
• Does it show action or state? → Verb
• Does it show relationship? → Preposition
• Does it connect words? → Conjunction
• Does it express emotion? → Interjection
