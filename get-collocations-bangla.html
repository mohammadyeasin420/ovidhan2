📖 Introduction: What Are "Get" Collocations?

"Get" is one of the most useful verbs in English. We use it for obtaining, receiving, becoming, and many everyday actions. ("Get" ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে দরকারী ক্রিয়াপদগুলির মধ্যে একটি। আমরা এটি প্রাপ্তি, গ্রহণ, হওয়া এবং অনেক দৈনন্দিন কাজের জন্য ব্যবহার করি।)

However, "get" doesn't go with every word. For example, we say "get a job" – not "take a job" (though that's also possible in some contexts). We say "get married" – not "make married". (তবে "get" প্রতিটি শব্দের সাথে যায় না। উদাহরণস্বরূপ, আমরা "get a job" বলি – "take a job" নয়।)

📝 "I need to get a job after graduation." – Sounds natural, right?
📝 "পাস করার পর আমার চাকরি পেতে হবে।" – স্বাভাবিক শোনায়, তাই না?
❌ "I need to do a job after graduation." – Not quite natural!
❌ "পাস করার পর আমার চাকরি করতে হবে।" – খুব স্বাভাবিক নয়!

In this guide, I'll walk you through 40+ "get" collocations organised by meaning, with Bangla translations and example sentences. Let's get started! (এই গাইডে, আমি আপনাকে ৪০+ "get" কলোকেশন শেখাবো, বাংলা অর্থ এবং উদাহরণ সহ। শুরু করা যাক!)

⚡ Get vs Take vs Make – The Key Difference

This is one of the most common questions students ask. Here's the simple rule: (এটি শিক্ষার্থীদের সবচেয়ে সাধারণ প্রশ্নগুলির মধ্যে একটি। এখানে সহজ নিয়মটি রয়েছে:)

GET = obtain, receive, or become
GET = প্রাপ্ত করা, গ্রহণ করা বা হওয়া
Examples: get a job, get married, get better
উদাহরণ: get a job, get married, get better

TAKE = perform an action, use transportation
TAKE = একটি কাজ সম্পাদন, পরিবহন ব্যবহার
Examples: take a shower, take a bus, take a break
উদাহরণ: take a shower, take a bus, take a break

MAKE = create, produce, or cause
MAKE = তৈরি, উৎপাদন বা ঘটানো
Examples: make a cake, make a decision, make a mistake
উদাহরণ: make a cake, make a decision, make a mistake
💡 Quick Memory Trick:
💡 দ্রুত মনে রাখার টিপ:
Get = you obtain or become (get a job, get tired)
Get = আপনি প্রাপ্ত হন বা হন (get a job, get tired)
Take = you do an action (take a shower, take a photo)
Take = আপনি একটি কাজ করেন (take a shower, take a photo)
Make = you create something (make a cake, make a decision)
Make = আপনি কিছু তৈরি করেন (make a cake, make a decision)

❌ Common Mistakes with "Get" Collocations

Many students make these common mistakes with "get". Let's look at them so you can avoid them. (অনেক শিক্ষার্থী "get" দিয়ে এই সাধারণ ভুলগুলি করে। আসুন সেগুলি দেখি যাতে আপনি সেগুলি এড়াতে পারেন।)

❌ "He married." (without "got")
✅ "He got married."

📖 Explanation: "Get married" is the standard collocation, not just "married".

❌ "I need to make a job."
✅ "I need to get a job."

📖 Explanation: "Job" uses "get", not "make".

❌ "She is angry." (without "got") – this is actually correct, but "got angry" is more common for sudden anger
✅ "She got angry." (for sudden anger)

📖 Explanation: "Get angry" is used for becoming angry suddenly.

❌ "I need to take a job."
✅ "I need to get a job." / "I need to take a job." (both possible, but "get a job" is more common)

📖 Explanation: Both are possible, but "get a job" is more natural in most contexts.

🎯 Exam Tips – BCS, IELTS & Bank Jobs

📘 For BCS & Bank Jobs

  • "Get" collocations frequently appear in error correction questions.
  • Remember: get is for obtaining and becoming; take is for actions and transportation; make is for creating.

  • Learn collocations in context – not just as isolated lists.
  • Use "get better", "get worse", "get ready" in your essays.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the meaning of "get collocations"? ▾
Get collocations are combinations of words where the verb "get" is used with a noun or phrase to create a common expression. For example, "get a job" means to find or obtain a job. These collocations are essential for speaking English naturally.
Why are "get" collocations important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Get collocations are frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and vocabulary sections. Mastering them can help you score higher.
What is the difference between "get" and "take" collocations? ▾
Get collocations often involve obtaining, receiving, or becoming (get a job, get married). Take collocations often involve transportation (take a bus), actions (take a shower), or time (take a break).
