📖 1. Introduction

It's and Its are two of the most commonly confused words in English. They sound exactly the same but have completely different meanings and uses. (It's এবং Its ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে সাধারণ দ্ব্যর্থক শব্দগুলির মধ্যে দুটি। এগুলি সম্পূর্ণ একই রকম শোনায় কিন্তু সম্পূর্ণ ভিন্ন অর্থ ও ব্যবহার রয়েছে।)

The rule is simple: It's is a contraction of "it is" or "it has", and Its is a possessive adjective meaning belonging to it. This guide will help you master this pair with clear explanations, examples, and memory tricks. (নিয়মটি সহজ: It's হলো "it is" বা "it has" এর সংক্ষিপ্ত রূপ, এবং Its হলো একটি অধিকারসূচক বিশেষণ যার অর্থ এটির।)

💡 Remember This

It's = it is or it has (contraction)

Its = belongs to it (possession)

📝 "It's raining outside." (It is – বৃষ্টি হচ্ছে)
📝 "বাইরে বৃষ্টি হচ্ছে।"
📝 "The cat licked its paw." (Possession – বিড়াল তার পা চাটল)
📝 "বিড়াল তার পা চাটল।"

👨‍🏫 The Golden Rule

It's = It is or It has (contraction)

Its = Belongs to it (possession)

It's = It is / It has | Its = এটির

🧠 4. Memory Trick – Never Forget Again

Use these simple tricks to remember the difference forever: (পার্থক্যটি চিরকাল মনে রাখতে এই সহজ কৌশলগুলি ব্যবহার করুন:)

💡 Memory Trick #1 – The Replacement Test

If you can replace it with "it is" or "it has", use it's.

If you can't, use its.

💡 Memory Trick #2 – The Apostrophe Rule

It's has an apostrophe – it's a contraction of two words.

Its has no apostrophe – it's a possessive adjective.

💡 Memory Trick #3 – Bangla Connection

It's = এটি ...ছে (when you mean "it is" or "it has")

Its = এটির (when you mean "belonging to it")

"বইটির নিজস্ব আকর্ষণ আছে।"

❌ 6. Common Mistakes Table

Here are the most common mistakes Bangladeshi students make with it's and its: (বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীরা it's এবং its নিয়ে সবচেয়ে বেশি যে ভুলগুলি করে:)

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy?
The dog wagged it's tail. The dog wagged its tail. "Its" shows possession (the tail belongs to the dog).
Its a beautiful day. It's a beautiful day. "It's" = it is (contraction).
The company lost it's reputation. The company lost its reputation. "Its" shows possession (the reputation belongs to the company).
Its been a long time. It's been a long time. "It's" = it has (contraction).
💡 Quick Test

If you can say "it is" or "it has" in the sentence, use it's. If not, use its.

❓ 10. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between it's and its? ▾
It's is a contraction of 'it is' or 'it has'. Its is a possessive adjective meaning belonging to it. For example: 'It's raining' (it is) and 'The cat licked its paw' (possession).
How can I remember the difference between it's and its? ▾
Use the memory trick: If you can replace it with 'it is' or 'it has', use it's. Otherwise, use its. For example: 'It's (it is) a good idea' vs 'The dog wagged its tail'.
Is 'its' ever used with an apostrophe? ▾
No, 'its' is always without an apostrophe when showing possession. The apostrophe form 'it's' is only used for the contraction of 'it is' or 'it has'.
Is it 'it's' or 'its' when meaning 'belonging to it'? ▾
It is 'its' (without an apostrophe) when meaning belonging to it. For example: 'The company lost its reputation.'
Is it's or its more common in BCS exams? ▾
Both are frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. The most common test is choosing between 'it's' (contraction) and 'its' (possession) in error correction and sentence completion.

📝 11. Summary

Mastering the difference between It's and Its is essential for clear communication and exam success. Here are the key takeaways:

  • It's = Contraction – it is / it has (এটি ...ছে)
  • Its = Possessive Adjective – belonging to it (এটির)
  • Memory Trick: Replace with "it is" or "it has" – if it fits, use it's
  • Apostrophe: It's has an apostrophe (contraction), its does not
  • Exam Focus: In BCS, IELTS, and Bank Jobs, the contraction vs possession rule is most tested
