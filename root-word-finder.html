📌 What is a Root Word?

A root word is the most basic part of a word. It carries the core meaning and cannot be broken down further. For example, "act" is the root of action, active, react, interaction. (মূল শব্দ একটি শব্দের সবচেয়ে মৌলিক অংশ। এটি মূল অর্থ বহন করে এবং আরও ভাঙা যায় না।)

🔗 What are Prefixes and Suffixes?

  • Prefixes are added to the beginning of a root (e.g., re- in react).
  • Suffixes are added to the end of a root (e.g., -ion in action).

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is a root word? ▾
A root word is the most basic part of a word. It carries the core meaning and cannot be broken down further. For example, 'act' is the root of 'action', 'active', and 'react'.
What are prefixes and suffixes? ▾
Prefixes are letters added to the beginning of a root word (e.g., 're-' in 'react'). Suffixes are letters added to the end (e.g., '-ion' in 'action'). Both change the meaning or grammatical function of the root.
Why are root words important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Root words help you understand the meaning of new words without memorizing them individually. This is essential for BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams where vocabulary is heavily tested.

Can I search for a derived word? ▾

Does this tool work offline? ▾

What is the difference between a root and a base word? ▾
A root word is the core part that cannot be broken down (e.g., 'dict'). A base word is a complete word that can stand alone (e.g., 'act'). In this tool, we focus on roots from Latin and Greek origins.
