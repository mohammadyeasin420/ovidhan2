📖 What Are Collocations?

Collocations are combinations of words that naturally go together in English. For example, we say "make a decision" – not "do a decision". We say "take a shower" – not "do a shower". (Collocations হল শব্দের এমন সংমিশ্রণ যা ইংরেজিতে স্বাভাবিকভাবে একসাথে ব্যবহৃত হয়।)

Learning collocations helps you:

  • Sound more natural – like a native speaker
  • Score higher in exams – BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs test collocations
  • Write and speak more fluently – without hesitation
  • Avoid common mistakes – like "do a mistake" instead of "make a mistake"
📝 Example: Instead of "She does a mistake" ❌
উদাহরণ: "She does a mistake" এর পরিবর্তে
✅ "She makes a mistake."
"She makes a mistake." বলুন।

❌ Common Collocation Mistakes

Many students make these collocation mistakes. Avoid them to sound more natural and score higher in exams.

❌ "She does a mistake."
✅ "She makes a mistake."

📖 Explanation: "Make" is used with "mistake", not "do".

❌ "I need to make my homework."
✅ "I need to do my homework."

📖 Explanation: "Do" is used with homework, not "make".

❌ "He has a big coffee."
✅ "He takes a big coffee." / "He drinks a big coffee."

📖 Explanation: "Take" or "drink" is used with coffee, not "have".

❌ "She gave me a strong advice."
✅ "She gave me good advice."

📖 Explanation: "Strong" doesn't collocate with "advice". Use "good" or "helpful".

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What are collocations in English? ▾
Collocations are combinations of words that naturally go together in English. For example, we say 'make a decision' not 'do a decision'. Learning collocations helps you sound more natural and fluent.
Why are collocations important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Collocations are frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and vocabulary sections. Mastering collocations can help you score higher.
What is the difference between 'make' and 'do' collocations? ▾
'Make' collocations are usually for creating or producing something (make a decision, make a mistake). 'Do' collocations are for activities, tasks, or duties (do homework, do business).
