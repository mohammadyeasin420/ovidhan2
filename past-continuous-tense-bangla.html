📖 1. Introduction

Past Continuous Tense (অতীত নিরন্তর কাল) is used to describe an action that was ongoing at a specific time in the past or was interrupted by another action. (Past Continuous Tense অতীতের কোনো নির্দিষ্ট সময়ে চলছিল এমন কাজ বা অন্য কোনো কাজ দ্বারা বাধাপ্রাপ্ত কাজ বোঝাতে ব্যবহৃত হয়।)

For Bangladeshi students, Past Continuous is often confused with Past Simple. The key difference is: Past Simple = completed action vs Past Continuous = ongoing action. (বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীদের জন্য Past Continuous প্রায়ই Past Simple-এর সাথে বিভ্রান্ত হয়। মূল পার্থক্য: Past Simple = সম্পন্ন কাজ, Past Continuous = চলমান কাজ।)

📚 2. Definition & Usage

Past Continuous Tense is used to describe:

  • An action that was ongoing at a specific time in the past.
    (অতীতের কোনো নির্দিষ্ট সময়ে চলছিল এমন কাজ।)
    Example: "I was reading at 8 PM last night."
  • An action that was interrupted by another past action.
    (অন্য কোনো অতীত কাজ দ্বারা বাধাপ্রাপ্ত কাজ।)
    Example: "I was sleeping when the phone rang."
  • Two or more simultaneous past actions.
    (দুটি বা ততোধিক সমান্তরাল অতীত কাজ।)
    Example: "While I was studying, my sister was watching TV."
  • Background actions in a story.
    (গল্পের পটভূমির কাজ।)
    Example: "The sun was shining, and birds were singing."
  • Annoying habits in the past (with "always").
    ("Always" দিয়ে অতীতের বিরক্তিকর অভ্যাস।)
    Example: "He was always complaining about his job."
👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Students remember this easily: Ongoing past = Past Continuous | Interrupted by = Past Simple

📐 3. Structure – Affirmative, Negative & Interrogative

🔹 Affirmative (হ্যাঁ বোধক)

Subject + was/were + Verb(ing)
SubjectBe VerbV-ingExample
IwasreadingI was reading at 8 PM.
YouwerereadingYou were reading at 8 PM.
HewaswritingHe was writing a letter.
ShewasplayingShe was playing cricket.
ItwasrainingIt was raining heavily.
WeweregoingWe were going to school.
TheywereeatingThey were eating rice.

Note: I/He/She/It → was | You/We/They → were

🔸 Negative (না বোধক)

Subject + was/were + not + Verb(ing)
SubjectBe VerbNotV-ingExample
IwasnotreadingI was not reading at 8 PM.
HewasnotwritingHe was not writing a letter.
TheywerenotgoingThey were not going to school.

Short forms: wasn't = was not, weren't = were not

🔹 Interrogative (প্রশ্ন বোধক)

Was/Were + Subject + Verb(ing)?
Be VerbSubjectV-ingExample
WasIreadingWas I reading at 8 PM?
WashewritingWas he writing a letter?
WeretheygoingWere they going to school?

When, while, as = Past Continuous clues. If you see these words, think about ongoing actions and interruptions!

⚡ 5. Mini Comparison – Past Continuous vs Past Simple

This is the most common confusion. Here's the clear difference: (এটি সবচেয়ে সাধারণ বিভ্রান্তি। এখানে স্পষ্ট পার্থক্য রয়েছে:)

FeaturePast SimplePast Continuous
Usage Completed action Ongoing action
Structure Subject + V2 Subject + was/were + V-ing
Example I read a book. I was reading a book.
Bangla আমি একটি বই পড়লাম। আমি একটি বই পড়ছিলাম।
Time Expression yesterday, last week when, while, at 8 PM
💡 Quick Memory Trick:
Completed → Past Simple  |  Ongoing → Past Continuous
সম্পন্ন → Past Simple  |  চলমান → Past Continuous

📋 6. Rules with Examples

Rule 1: Ongoing Action at a Specific Past Time

✅ "I was reading at 8 PM last night."
✅ "আমি গত রাত ৮টায় পড়ছিলাম।"
✅ "She was cooking when I arrived."
✅ "আমি পৌঁছানোর সময় সে রান্না করছিল।"

Rule 2: Interrupted Action

✅ "I was sleeping when the phone rang."
✅ "ফোন বাজানোর সময় আমি ঘুমাচ্ছিলাম।"
✅ "He was driving when the accident happened."
✅ "দুর্ঘটনাটি ঘটার সময় সে গাড়ি চালাচ্ছিল।"

Rule 3: Parallel Actions (Two Simultaneous Actions)

✅ "While I was studying, my sister was watching TV."
✅ "আমি পড়াশোনা করছিলাম, আর আমার বোন টিভি দেখছিল।"
✅ "He was singing while she was playing the piano."
✅ "সে গান গাইছিল যখন সে পিয়ানো বাজাচ্ছিল।"

Rule 4: Background Description in Stories

✅ "The sun was shining, and the birds were singing."
✅ "সূর্য জ্বলছিল, এবং পাখিরা গান গাইছিল।"

Rule 5: Annoying Habits (with "always")

✅ "He was always coming late to class."
✅ "সে সবসময় ক্লাসে দেরিতে আসত (বিরক্তিকর অভ্যাস)।"

❌ 7. Common Mistakes – Bangladeshi Students

❌ "I was go to school when he came."
✅ "I was going to school when he came."

📖 After "was/were", always use the -ing form. "Go" → "going".

❌ "They was playing cricket."
✅ "They were playing cricket."

📖 "They" takes "were", not "was". Remember: I/He/She/It → was, You/We/They → were.

❌ "He was read a book."
✅ "He was reading a book."

📖 Always use the -ing form after "was/were". "Read" → "reading".

❌ "I was not went to school."
✅ "I was not going to school."

📖 Negative sentences also need the -ing form. "Went" → "going".

🔄 8. Common Bangla Translation Errors

These are mistakes Bangladeshi students often make when translating from Bangla to English. (বাংলা থেকে ইংরেজিতে অনুবাদ করার সময় বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীরা প্রায়ই এই ভুলগুলি করে।)

❌ তার আসার সময় আমি পড়ছিলাম।
❌ "I read when he came."
✅ "I was reading when he came."

📖 "আসার সময়" (when he came) indicates an ongoing action – use Past Continuous for the longer action.

❌ সে যখন এলো, আমি খাচ্ছিলাম।
❌ "I ate when he came."
✅ "I was eating when he came."

📖 The ongoing action (eating) uses Past Continuous. The interrupting action (came) uses Past Simple.

❌ আমি পড়ছিলাম আর সে লিখছিল।
❌ "I read and he wrote."
✅ "I was reading while he was writing."

📖 Two simultaneous past actions → both use Past Continuous.

🚫 11. When NOT to Use Past Continuous

Past Continuous is not used for: (Past Continuous ব্যবহার করা হয় না:)

  • Completed past actions – Use Past Simple instead.
  • Stative verbs (feelings, thoughts, senses, possession) – Use Past Simple instead.
  • Habitual past actions – Use Past Simple or "used to".
❌ "I was knowing him."
✅ "I knew him."

📖 "Know" is a stative verb – it is NOT used in continuous form.

❌ "I was going to school yesterday."
✅ "I went to school yesterday."

📖 "Yesterday" with a completed action → Past Simple.

👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Remember: Past Continuous is for ongoing actions. If the action is completed or a state, use Past Simple.

👨‍🏫 13. Teacher Tips

💡 Tip 1 – Was vs Were

I/He/She/It → was | You/We/They → were. Practice with flashcards to remember!

💡 Tip 2 – Interrupted Actions

Longer action = Past Continuous | Shorter interrupting action = Past Simple. Example: "I was sleeping when the phone rang."

💡 Tip 3 – Signal Words

When, while, as – these words are your clues for Past Continuous. If you see them, look for an ongoing action!

🎯 14. BCS & Bank Job Section

📘 Previous BCS Patterns

  • Error Correction: Identify and correct "was/were" + V2 errors (should be V-ing).
  • Sentence Completion: Fill in the blank with the correct Past Continuous form.
  • Transformation: Change sentences to show interrupted actions.
Example 1 (Error Correction):
❌ "I was go to school when he came."
✅ "I was going to school when he came."
Example 2 (Sentence Completion):
"She ______ (study) when I called her."
✅ "She was studying when I called her."
Example 3 (Transformation):
"I read a book. He came."
✅ "I was reading a book when he came."

💡 Tips for BCS

  • Remember: was/were + V-ing (not V2).
  • Look for signal words: when, while, as
  • If an action is interrupted, the longer action is Past Continuous.

🌍 15. IELTS Section

✍️ Writing Tips

  • Use Past Continuous to describe background actions in Task 2 essays.
  • Example: "While the government was implementing new policies, the economy was improving."
  • Use Past Continuous to show interrupted actions in narratives.
  • Example: "I was walking home when I saw the accident."

🗣️ Speaking Tips

  • Use Past Continuous to describe past experiences vividly.
  • Example: "I was waiting for the bus when I met my old friend."
  • Use Past Continuous to set the scene in Part 2 of the Speaking test.
  • Example: "The sun was shining, and people were enjoying the day."
Band 6 Example: "I read a book when he came."

❓ 19. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Past Continuous Tense in Bangla? ▾
Past Continuous Tense (অতীত নিরন্তর কাল) is used to describe an action that was ongoing at a specific time in the past. For example: 'I was reading when he came' – তার আসার সময় আমি পড়ছিলাম।
What is the structure of Past Continuous Tense? ▾
The structure is: Subject + was/were + Verb(ing). For example: I was reading, You were reading, He was reading. Negative: Subject + was/were + not + V-ing. Interrogative: Was/Were + Subject + V-ing?
What is the difference between Past Continuous and Past Simple? ▾
Past Simple is used for a completed past action (I went). Past Continuous is used for an ongoing past action that was interrupted (I was going when he came).
How do we form negative sentences in Past Continuous? ▾
We use 'was not' (wasn't) with I/He/She/It and 'were not' (weren't) with You/We/They + the -ing form. For example: 'I was not reading' or 'I wasn't reading.'
What are the common signal words for Past Continuous? ▾
Common signal words are: when, while, as, at that time, at 8 PM, all day, the whole morning, constantly, always. These words help identify ongoing past actions.
Why is Past Continuous important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Past Continuous is frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and transformation. It's especially important for showing interrupted actions in the past.
Can we use 'was' and 'were' with all subjects? ▾
Yes, but remember: I/He/She/It → was. You/We/They → were. This is the same as Present Continuous but in the past.

📝 20. Summary

Past Continuous Tense is used to describe ongoing past actions. Here are the key takeaways:

  • Structure: Subject + was/were + V-ing
  • Negative: Subject + was/were + not + V-ing
  • Interrogative: Was/Were + Subject + V-ing?
  • Usage: Ongoing past actions, interrupted actions, parallel actions, background description, annoying habits
  • Signal Words: when, while, as, at that time, at 8 PM, all day
  • Avoid: Using stative verbs (know, believe, etc.) in continuous form
  • Key Difference: Past Simple = completed | Past Continuous = ongoing
💡 Golden Rule

"Was/were + V-ing for ongoing actions. V2 for completed actions that interrupt."

"চলমান কাজের জন্য was/were + V-ing। বাধা দেওয়া কাজের জন্য V2।"
