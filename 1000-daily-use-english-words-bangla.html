❌ Common Vocabulary Mistakes

Many students make these common mistakes. Avoid them to sound more natural and score higher in exams.

❌ "I am agree with you."
✅ "I agree with you."

📖 Explanation: "Agree" is a verb – we don't use "am" with it. Just say "I agree".

❌ "She is very beautifully."
✅ "She is very beautiful."

📖 Explanation: After "is", we use an adjective ("beautiful"), not an adverb ("beautifully").

❌ "I have a important meeting."
✅ "I have an important meeting."

📖 Explanation: "Important" starts with a vowel sound → use "an".

🎯 Exam Tips – BCS, IELTS, HSC & SSC

📘 For BCS & Bank Jobs

  • Focus on synonyms and antonyms – they ask at least 5-10 questions on these.
  • Learn word families (e.g., beauty, beautiful, beautifully, beautify).
  • Pay attention to collocations (e.g., make a decision, not do a decision).

🌍 For IELTS

  • Use a variety of vocabulary in your writing and speaking tasks.
  • Learn words in context – don't just memorize lists.
  • Practice using synonyms to avoid repetition.

🎒 For HSC & SSC

  • Master the first 500 words from this list – they appear most frequently.
  • Practice writing sentences with each word.
  • Review the Bangla meanings to ensure you understand fully.

🧠 Memory Tricks to Remember 1000 Words

  • Mnemonic: For "beautiful" – think "bees are beautiful".
  • Bangla Trick: Connect English words with similar-sounding Bangla words.
  • Visualization: Picture the word in action. For "run", imagine someone running.
  • Group by Theme: Learn words in groups – food, family, school, office, travel.
  • Spaced Repetition: Review words regularly using flashcards. Learn 10 new words daily and review yesterday's words.
💡 Tip: Use the word in a sentence immediately after learning it.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How many daily use English words should I learn? ▾
For basic daily conversation, 500-1000 words are enough. For BCS, IELTS, and university admission exams, aim for 2,000-3,000 words. This list of 1000 daily use words is the perfect starting point.
What is the best way to memorize daily use English words? ▾
The best way is to group words by themes, use flashcards, and practice them in sentences daily. Try to use 5-10 new words every day in your writing or speaking. Consistent practice beats cramming.
Are these daily use English words useful for BCS preparation? ▾
Yes. These 1000 daily use words form the foundation of BCS vocabulary. Many of these words frequently appear in BCS Preliminary and Written exams. Master them before moving to advanced BCS vocabulary.
Can I use this list for IELTS preparation? ▾
Absolutely. These 1000 words cover everyday topics that appear in IELTS Speaking and Writing tasks. For higher bands (7+), you should also learn academic and less common words, but this list is your starting point.
How long does it take to learn 1000 English words? ▾
If you learn 10 words per day, it will take 100 days (about 3 months). If you learn 20 words per day, it will take 50 days (about 2 months). Consistency is more important than speed.
Should I learn synonyms and antonyms with each word? ▾
Yes. Learning synonyms and antonyms helps you understand the word better and expands your vocabulary faster. This is especially useful for BCS and IELTS exams where synonym/antonym questions are common.
Is it better to learn English words with Bangla meaning or English meaning? ▾
For Bangladeshi students, learning with Bangla meaning is the most effective way to start. As you become more advanced, you can transition to English-to-English definitions. This list provides clear Bangla meanings for every word.
What should I do after learning these 1000 daily use words? ▾
After mastering these 1000 words, move on to academic vocabulary, BCS-specific vocabulary, IELTS vocabulary, and finally, advanced and business vocabulary. Every new word you learn should be added to your daily practice.
