Introduction – Why Grammar Matters

Learning English grammar is the foundation of speaking, writing, reading, and understanding English correctly. Whether you are preparing for BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs, University Admission, or simply improving your communication skills, grammar is one of the most important subjects to master.

This complete English Grammar for Beginners in Bangla guide has been created especially for Bangladeshi students. Every lesson uses simple English together with Bangla explanations so that beginners can understand grammar without confusion.

Instead of memorizing difficult rules, this guide focuses on practical learning with examples, common mistakes, and everyday usage. By studying one section at a time and practicing regularly, you can gradually build confidence in English.

📌 What you will learn in this guide:
  • Basic English grammar rules
  • Parts of Speech explained simply
  • Easy Bangla examples
  • Common grammar mistakes
  • Sentence formation
  • Grammar for IELTS, BCS and job preparation

Parts of Speech (পদের প্রকারভেদ)

Every English sentence is made up of different kinds of words. These different kinds of words are called Parts of Speech. Understanding Parts of Speech is the first step toward mastering English grammar.

বাংলায় বলতে গেলে, বাক্যে ব্যবহৃত প্রতিটি শব্দের একটি নির্দিষ্ট কাজ থাকে। সেই কাজ অনুযায়ী শব্দগুলোকে ৮টি ভাগে ভাগ করা হয়।

Part of Speech বাংলা অর্থ Example
Nounবিশেষ্যRahim, Book, Dhaka
Pronounসর্বনামHe, She, They
Verbক্রিয়াGo, Eat, Write
AdjectiveবিশেষণBeautiful, Tall
Adverbক্রিয়া বিশেষণQuickly, Slowly
Prepositionপদান্বয়ী অব্যয়In, On, At
ConjunctionসংযোজকAnd, But, Because
Interjectionআবেগসূচক শব্দWow!, Oh!, Alas!

Noun (বিশেষ্য)

A Noun is the name of a person, place, animal, thing, or idea.

বাংলায়, যে শব্দ দ্বারা ব্যক্তি, স্থান, বস্তু, প্রাণী বা ধারণার নাম বোঝায় তাকে বিশেষ্য (Noun) বলে।

EnglishBangla

Pronoun (সর্বনাম)

A Pronoun is used instead of a noun.

একই Noun বারবার ব্যবহার না করে তার পরিবর্তে যে শব্দ ব্যবহার করা হয় তাকে Pronoun বলে।

NounPronoun
RahimHe
Karim and RahimThey
The bookIt
FatimaShe
You and IWe

Examples

Rahim is a student. He studies English.
রহিম একজন ছাত্র। সে ইংরেজি পড়ে।

The children are playing. They are very happy.

She is a teacher at the university.

We are learning English grammar.

Verb (ক্রিয়া)

A Verb expresses an action, condition, or state.

যে শব্দ দ্বারা কাজ, অবস্থা বা ঘটনার প্রকাশ ঘটে তাকে Verb বলে।

Speakকথা বলাHe speaks English.
LearnশেখাThey learn grammar.
Runদৌড়ানোHe runs fast.

Adjective (বিশেষণ)

An Adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun.

বাংলায়, যে শব্দ Noun বা Pronoun-এর গুণ, দোষ, সংখ্যা, পরিমাণ বা অবস্থা প্রকাশ করে তাকে বিশেষণ (Adjective) বলে।

Adjective Bangla Meaning Example Sentence

An Adverb modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

যে শব্দ Verb, Adjective অথবা অন্য Adverb-এর অর্থকে আরও স্পষ্ট করে তাকে Adverb বলে।

Preposition (পদান্বয়ী অব্যয়)

A Preposition shows the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and another word in a sentence.

বাংলায়, Noun বা Pronoun-এর সাথে বাক্যের অন্য শব্দের সম্পর্ক প্রকাশকারী শব্দকে Preposition বলে।

WithসাথেShe goes with her friend.

Conjunction (সংযোজক)

A Conjunction joins words, phrases, or sentences.

যে শব্দ দুইটি শব্দ, বাক্যাংশ অথবা বাক্যকে যুক্ত করে তাকে Conjunction বলে।

Conjunction Bangla Meaning Example
AndএবংI study English and Mathematics.
Butকিন্তুHe is poor but honest.
OrঅথবাTea or coffee?
BecauseকারণStay home because it is raining.
AlthoughযদিওAlthough he is rich, he is humble.
SoতাইHe worked hard, so he passed.
IfযদিIf you study, you will pass.
WhenযখনWhen I arrived, he was sleeping.
WhileযখনWhile I was reading, the phone rang.
Unlessযদি নাYou cannot pass unless you study.

An Interjection expresses sudden emotion or feeling.

হঠাৎ আনন্দ, দুঃখ, বিস্ময় বা আবেগ প্রকাশকারী শব্দকে Interjection বলে।

Tenses (কাল)

Tense shows the time of an action. There are three main tenses:

  • Present Tense – বর্তমান কাল
  • Past Tense – অতীত কাল
  • Future Tense – ভবিষ্যত কাল
Tense Structure Example
Present Indefinite Subject + Verb (s/es) He goes to school.
Present Continuous Subject + am/is/are + Verb(ing) He is going to school.
Past Indefinite Subject + Verb (2nd form) He went to school.
Past Continuous Subject + was/were + Verb(ing) He was going to school.
Future Indefinite Subject + will + Verb He will go to school.
Future Continuous Subject + will be + Verb(ing) He will be going to school.

For a complete guide to Tenses, visit our Detailed Tense Guide.

Articles (a, an, the)

Articles are used before nouns. There are three articles:

  • a – used before consonant sounds (a boy, a university)
  • an – used before vowel sounds (an hour, an honest man)
  • the – used for specific nouns (the sun, the best student)
ArticleWhen to UseExample
aConsonant sounda book, a tree, a university
anVowel soundan apple, an hour, an honest man
theSpecific / Uniquethe sun, the moon, the best student

Common Grammar Mistakes to Avoid

MistakeCorrectionBangla Explanation
I am agree I agree Agree verb-এর সাথে am বসে না
She don't know She doesn't know Third person singular → doesn't
He go to office He goes to office Third person singular → goes
I have a work I have work Work uncountable noun
It's raining since morning It has been raining since morning Since → Present Perfect Continuous
He is senior than me He is senior to me Senior এর পরে to হয়, than নয়
I am knowing I know Know-এর continuous tense হয় না
