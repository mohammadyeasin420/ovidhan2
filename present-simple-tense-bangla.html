📖 1. Introduction

Present Simple Tense (সাধারণ বর্তমান কাল) is the most basic and frequently used tense in English. It describes habits, facts, routines, and fixed arrangements. (Present Simple Tense ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে মৌলিক এবং ব্যবহৃত টেন্স। এটি অভ্যাস, সত্য, দৈনন্দিন কাজ এবং নির্দিষ্ট সময়সূচী বোঝায়।)

For Bangladeshi students, this is usually the first tense they learn. However, mistakes often happen with subject-verb agreement – especially adding 's' or 'es' for third person singular. This guide will clear up all confusion. (বাংলাদেশী শিক্ষার্থীদের জন্য Present Simple সাধারণত প্রথম টেন্স। তবে, subject-verb agreement নিয়ে প্রায়ই ভুল হয় – বিশেষ করে third person singular-এর জন্য 's' বা 'es' যোগ করা নিয়ে।)

📚 2. Definition & Usage

Present Simple Tense is used to describe:

  • Habitual actions or routines.
    (অভ্যাসগত কাজ বা দৈনন্দিন রুটিন।)
    Example: "I wake up at 6 AM every day."
  • Universal truths or general facts.
    (চিরন্তন সত্য বা সাধারণ ঘটনা।)
    Example: "The sun rises in the east."
  • Fixed schedules or timetables.
    (নির্দিষ্ট সময়সূচী।)
    Example: "The train leaves at 8 PM."
  • Feelings, thoughts, and opinions.
    (অনুভূতি, চিন্তা এবং মতামত।)
    Example: "I think she is right."
  • Instructions or directions.
    (নির্দেশনা বা দিকনির্দেশ।)
    Example: "First, you mix the ingredients."
👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Students remember this easily: Yesterday → Past Simple | Now → Present Continuous | Every day → Present Simple

📐 3. Structure – Affirmative, Negative & Interrogative

🔹 Affirmative (হ্যাঁ বোধক)

Subject + Verb (base form)
For He/She/It: Subject + Verb + s/es
SubjectVerbExample
IreadI read books.
YoureadYou read books.
HereadsHe reads books.
ShewritesShe writes letters.
ItworksIt works well.
WeplayWe play cricket.
TheygoThey go to school.

🔸 Negative (না বোধক)

Subject + do/does + not + Verb (base form)
SubjectAuxiliaryNotV1Example
IdonotreadI do not read books.
HedoesnotreadHe does not read books.
TheydonotgoThey do not go to school.

Short forms: don't = do not, doesn't = does not

❌ He doesn't goes to school.
✅ He doesn't go to school.

📖 After "do/does not", always use the base form (V1) – never add 's' or 'es'!

🔹 Interrogative (প্রশ্ন বোধক)

Do/Does + Subject + Verb (base form)?
AuxiliarySubjectV1Example
DoyoureadDo you read books?
DoeshereadDoes he read books?
DotheygoDo they go to school?
❌ Does he reads books?
✅ Does he read books?

📖 After "Does", always use the base form (V1) – never add 's' or 'es'!

📝 4. Rules for Adding 's' and 'es' – Third Person Singular

For He, She, It and singular nouns, we add 's' or 'es' to the verb. (He, She, It এবং singular noun-এর জন্য verb-এর শেষে 's' বা 'es' যোগ করতে হয়।)

RuleBase VerbThird PersonExample
Add 's' – Most verbs play, work, read plays, works, reads He plays cricket.
Add 'es' – Verbs ending in -o, -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -z go, watch, wash, kiss, fix goes, watches, washes, kisses, fixes She watches TV.
Change y → i + es – Verbs ending in consonant + y study, cry, fly studies, cries, flies He studies English.
Add 's' – Verbs ending in vowel + y play, say, buy plays, says, buys She plays the piano.
Irregular have, do has, does He has a car.
💡 Memory Trick

"He goes, she watches, it studies – never forget the 's' and 'es'!"

These words almost always indicate Present Simple Tense. (এই শব্দগুলি প্রায় সবসময় Present Simple Tense নির্দেশ করে।)

Signal WordMeaning (বাংলা)Example

usuallyসাধারণতShe usually drinks tea.
oftenপ্রায়ইWe often go to the park.

neverকখনও নাI never tell lies.

👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Adverbs of frequency (always, usually, often, sometimes, never) are placed before the main verb. Example: "I always read books."

⚡ 6. Mini Comparison – Present Simple vs Present Continuous vs Present Perfect

Understanding the differences between these tenses is crucial for exam success. (পরীক্ষায় সফল হওয়ার জন্য এই টেন্সগুলির মধ্যে পার্থক্য বোঝা অত্যন্ত গুরুত্বপূর্ণ।)

TenseUsageExampleBangla
Present Simple Habits, facts, routines I work every day. আমি প্রতিদিন কাজ করি।
Present Continuous Actions happening now I am working now. আমি এখন কাজ করছি।
Present Perfect Past with present result I have worked here. আমি এখানে কাজ করেছি।
💡 Quick Memory Trick:
Every day → Present Simple  |  Now → Present Continuous  |  Already → Present Perfect
প্রতিদিন → Present Simple  |  এখন → Present Continuous  |  ইতিমধ্যে → Present Perfect

🚫 12. When NOT to Use Present Simple

Present Simple is not used for actions happening right now. Use Present Continuous instead. (Present Simple এখন চলছে এমন কাজের জন্য ব্যবহার করা হয় না। পরিবর্তে Present Continuous ব্যবহার করুন।)

❌ "I read a book now."
✅ "I am reading a book now."

📖 "Now" indicates an action happening at this moment → use Present Continuous.

❌ "She watches TV at the moment."
✅ "She is watching TV at the moment."

📖 "At the moment" indicates an action happening now → use Present Continuous.

👨‍🏫 Teacher Tip

Remember: If the action is happening right now, use Present Continuous. If it's a habit or routine, use Present Simple.

👨‍🏫 14. Teacher Tips

💡 Tip 1 – Signal Words

Every day, always, usually, sometimes, never → These are your clues for Present Simple. If you see them, use Present Simple!

💡 Tip 2 – Subject-Verb Agreement

He, She, It → add 's' or 'es'. I, You, We, They → base form. This is the most important rule!

💡 Tip 3 – Common Exam Trap

In exams, they often test: "Does" + V1 (not V1+s) and "doesn't" + V1 (not V1+s). Remember this and you'll score higher!

❓ 20. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Present Simple Tense in Bangla? ▾
Present Simple Tense (সাধারণ বর্তমান কাল) is used to describe habitual actions, general truths, and fixed arrangements. For example: "I read books" – আমি বই পড়ি।
What is the structure of Present Simple Tense? ▾
The structure is: Subject + Verb (base form) for I/You/We/They. For He/She/It: Subject + Verb + s/es. For negative: Subject + do/does + not + V1. For interrogative: Do/Does + Subject + V1?
When do we add 's' or 'es' to verbs in Present Simple? ▾
We add 's' or 'es' when the subject is He, She, It, or any singular noun. For verbs ending in -o, -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, we add 'es' (go → goes, watch → watches). For verbs ending in consonant + y, we change y to i and add 'es' (study → studies).
What is the difference between Present Simple and Present Continuous? ▾
Present Simple is used for habitual actions, general truths, and fixed schedules (I go to school every day). Present Continuous is used for actions happening right now or temporary actions (I am going to school now).
How do we form negative sentences in Present Simple? ▾
We use 'do not' (don't) with I/You/We/They and 'does not' (doesn't) with He/She/It + the base form of the verb. For example: 'I do not go' or 'He does not go.' Never use 'does not' with 's' (e.g., 'He does not goes' is wrong).
What are the common signal words for Present Simple? ▾
Common signal words are: always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day, every week, every month, on Mondays, at the weekend, in the morning.
Why is Present Simple important for BCS and IELTS? ▾
Present Simple is one of the most frequently tested tenses in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. Questions appear in error correction, sentence completion, and transformation. Subject-verb agreement with 's/es' is especially important.

📝 21. Summary

Present Simple Tense is a fundamental tense used to describe habits, facts, and routines. Here are the key takeaways:

  • Structure: Subject + V1 (I/You/We/They) | Subject + V1+s/es (He/She/It)
  • Negative: Subject + do/does + not + V1
  • Interrogative: Do/Does + Subject + V1?
  • Usage: Habits, universal truths, fixed schedules, opinions, instructions
  • Signal Words: always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day, on Mondays
  • Avoid: Adding 's' or 'es' after "do/does" (e.g., "does goes" is wrong)
💡 Golden Rule

"For He, She, It – add 's' or 'es'. For I, You, We, They – use the base form."

"He, She, It-এর জন্য 's' বা 'es' যোগ করুন। I, You, We, They-এর জন্য base form ব্যবহার করুন।"
