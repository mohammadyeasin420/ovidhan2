📖 1. Introduction

Then and Than are two of the most commonly confused words in English. They sound similar but have completely different meanings and uses. (Then এবং Than ইংরেজির সবচেয়ে সাধারণ দ্ব্যর্থক শব্দগুলির মধ্যে দুটি। এগুলি একই রকম শোনায় কিন্তু সম্পূর্ণ ভিন্ন অর্থ ও ব্যবহার রয়েছে।)

The good news is that the rule is very simple: Then is used for time or sequence, and Than is used for comparison. (সুসংবাদটি হলো যে নিয়মটি খুব সহজ: Then ব্যবহার করা হয় সময় বা ক্রম বোঝাতে, এবং Than ব্যবহার করা হয় তুলনা বোঝাতে।)

📝 "I ate then I slept." (Time/Sequence)
📝 "আমি খেলাম তারপর ঘুমালাম।" (সময়/ক্রম)
📝 "She is taller than me." (Comparison)
📝 "সে আমার চেয়ে লম্বা।" (তুলনা)

📚 2. Definition – Then vs Than

Then
(Adverb)
তারপর, তখন
Used for time, sequence, or consequence
Than
(Conjunction/Preposition)
চেয়ে, তুলনায়
Used for comparison between two things
👨‍🏫 The Golden Rule

Then = Time / Sequence | Than = Comparison

Then = সময় / ক্রম | Than = তুলনা

FeatureThenThan

Test Can you replace it with "at that time"? Is it comparing two things?

🧠 4. Memory Trick – Never Forget Again

Use these simple tricks to remember the difference forever: (পার্থক্যটি চিরকাল মনে রাখতে এই সহজ কৌশলগুলি ব্যবহার করুন:)

💡 Memory Trick #1 – The T & C Rule

Then = Time (both start with T)

Than = Comparison (has a 'c' sound)

💡 Memory Trick #2 – The Vowel Rule

Then has an e – like event or sequence.

Than has an a – like apply or compare.

💡 Memory Trick #3 – Bangla Connection

Then = তারপর (time) – when you need "por" (later).

Than = চেয়ে (comparison) – when you need "cheye".

📝 5. Examples with Bangla Translation

Here are more examples to help you understand the difference: (পার্থক্য বোঝার জন্য এখানে আরও উদাহরণ দেওয়া হলো:)

🔹 Then (Time / Sequence)

1. "I woke up at 6 AM, then I went for a walk."
"আমি সকাল ৬টায় ঘুম থেকে উঠলাম, তারপর হাঁটতে গেলাম।"
2. "First, we eat. Then, we sleep."
"প্রথমে, আমরা খাই। তারপর, আমরা ঘুমাই।"
3. "I was at home then."
"আমি তখন বাড়িতে ছিলাম।"
4. "He arrived and then left."
"সে এসেছিল এবং তারপর চলে গেল।"
5. "If you are ready, then we can go."
"যদি তুমি প্রস্তুত হও, তাহলে আমরা যেতে পারি।"

🔸 Than (Comparison)

1. "She is smarter than her brother."
"সে তার ভাইয়ের চেয়ে বেশি বুদ্ধিমান।"
2. "This book is better than that one."
"এই বইটি ঐটির চেয়ে ভালো।"
3. "I work harder than you."
"আমি তোমার চেয়ে বেশি কাজ করি।"
4. "He earns more money than me."
"সে আমার চেয়ে বেশি টাকা উপার্জন করে।"
5. "She arrived earlier than expected."
"সে প্রত্যাশার চেয়ে তাড়াতাড়ি পৌঁছেছিল।"

🔹 Side-by-Side Comparison

Then: "I studied, then I took a break."
"আমি পড়াশোনা করলাম, তারপর বিরতি নিলাম।"
Than: "I studied more than I did yesterday."
"আমি গতকালের চেয়ে বেশি পড়াশোনা করলাম।"

🎯 7. Exam Tips – BCS, IELTS & Bank Jobs

📘 For BCS & Bank Jobs

  • Then vs Than is frequently tested in error correction and sentence completion.
  • Most questions test the basic rule: then = time/sequence, than = comparison.
  • Look for clues: If there's a comparison (taller, smarter, better), use than.
  • If there's a sequence (first...then, after...then), use then.

🌍 For IELTS

  • Using then and than correctly improves your lexical resource score.
  • In writing, avoid common errors – it shows advanced command of English.
  • In speaking, natural use of these words shows fluency and accuracy.

🎒 For HSC & SSC

  • Board exams often test this pair in grammar sections.
  • Remember the T & C rule: Then = Time, Than = Comparison.
BCS Example:
"He is smarter ______ his brother."

❓ 9. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between then and than? ▾
Then is used for time or sequence (e.g., 'I ate then I slept'). Than is used for comparison (e.g., 'She is taller than me').
How can I remember the difference between then and than? ▾
Use the memory trick: Then = Time (both start with T), Than = Comparison (has a 'c' sound). Or remember: 'Then' has 'e' for 'event' or 'sequence', 'Than' has 'a' for 'compare'.
Can 'than' be used for time? ▾
No, 'than' is never used for time. It is only used for comparison. If you are talking about time or sequence, always use 'then'.
Can 'then' be used for comparison? ▾
No, 'then' is never used for comparison. It is only used for time or sequence. If you are comparing two things, always use 'than'.
Is then or than more common in BCS exams? ▾
Both are frequently tested in BCS, IELTS, and Bank Job exams. The most common test is choosing between 'then' (time) and 'than' (comparison) in sentence completion and error correction.

📝 10. Summary

Mastering the difference between Then and Than is essential for clear communication and exam success. Here are the key takeaways:

  • Then = Time / Sequence – use for order of events
  • Than = Comparison – use for comparing two things
  • Memory Trick: T = Time (Then) | C = Comparison (Than)
  • Bangla Connection: Then = তারপর | Than = চেয়ে
  • Exam Focus: In BCS, IELTS, and Bank Jobs, the time/comparison rule is most tested
💡 Golden Rule

"If it's time or sequence, use then. If it's a comparison, use than."
