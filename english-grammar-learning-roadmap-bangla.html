📌 Introduction – Why a Roadmap is Important

Learning English grammar without a plan is like traveling without a map. You might get somewhere, but it will take longer and you might miss important stops along the way.

This 12-week roadmap is designed to take you from a complete beginner to a confident English grammar user. Whether you're preparing for BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs, University Admission, or just improving your English, this roadmap will guide you step by step.

📖 How to Use This Roadmap:
  • Follow the order – Topics are arranged logically
  • Study daily – Consistency is more important than speed
  • Practice regularly – Use the tools and quizzes
  • Track your progress – Use the Ovidhan Dashboard
  • Revise weekly – Review what you learned
🎯 Three Phases:
  • Beginner (Weeks 1-4): Foundation – Parts of Speech, Sentence Structure, Subject-Verb Agreement, Tenses Basics
  • Intermediate (Weeks 5-8): Core Grammar – Tenses Advanced, Articles, Prepositions, Voice, Narration
  • Advanced (Weeks 9-12): Mastery – Transformation, Right Form of Verb, Advanced Topics, Revision & Practice

Let's start your journey!

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to learn English grammar?
With consistent daily practice (1-2 hours), you can learn the basics in 2-3 months. To master advanced grammar concepts, it may take 6-12 months of regular study and practice. This 12-week roadmap will help you build a strong foundation.
Where should I start learning grammar?
Start with Parts of Speech (Nouns, Pronouns, Verbs, Adjectives, Adverbs, Prepositions, Conjunctions, Interjections). This forms the foundation of all grammar. Then move to Sentence Structure, Subject-Verb Agreement, and Tenses.
How many hours should I practice daily?
For beginners, 1-2 hours daily is recommended. This should include 30 minutes of reading/studying, 30 minutes of practice exercises, and 30 minutes of revision. Consistency is more important than long hours.
Can I learn grammar without a teacher?
Yes, you can learn grammar independently using quality resources. This roadmap provides all the lessons you need, and the Ovidhan app offers practice exercises, quizzes, and tools. The key is consistent self-study and practice.
What is the best order to learn grammar topics?
The best order is: Parts of Speech → Sentence Structure → Subject-Verb Agreement → Tenses → Articles → Prepositions → Voice Change → Narration → Transformation → Right Form of Verb. This roadmap follows this exact logical sequence.
How can I practice grammar daily?
Follow the daily practice routine: Read one grammar lesson, solve 10-15 practice questions, take a quiz, and review your mistakes. Use the Ovidhan app's grammar tools and exercises for consistent practice.
Is grammar important for IELTS?
Yes, grammar is very important for IELTS. Writing Task 1 & 2 require accurate grammar usage. Speaking requires correct grammar for a higher band score. This roadmap covers all the grammar topics needed for IELTS preparation.
Is grammar important for BCS and Bank Jobs?
Yes, grammar is extremely important for BCS and Bank Job exams. Subject-Verb Agreement, Tenses, Voice Change, Narration, Articles, and Prepositions are frequently tested. This roadmap includes exam-focused preparation.
How do I stay motivated while learning grammar?
Set small daily goals, track your progress on the Ovidhan Dashboard, celebrate small wins, and practice with interactive tools and quizzes. The gamification features (XP, levels, achievements) can help keep you motivated.
What are the most difficult grammar topics?
The most difficult topics for learners are: Subject-Verb Agreement (especially with indefinite pronouns), Tenses (knowing which tense to use), Voice Change, Narration (tense changes), and Prepositions. This roadmap allocates more time to these topics.
How do I know if I'm making progress?
Track your progress using the Ovidhan Dashboard. Complete quizzes and exercises to test your knowledge. If your quiz scores are improving and you're making fewer mistakes, you're making progress. Regular revision is also important.
What should I do after finishing the 12-week plan?
After completing this roadmap, you should have a strong grammar foundation. Next steps: Take advanced grammar quizzes, practice writing essays, read English books and articles, and prepare for specific exams (BCS, IELTS, Bank Jobs).
How can I improve grammar for spoken English?
Focus on Parts of Speech, Sentence Structure, Tenses, and Subject-Verb Agreement for spoken English. Practice speaking daily, record yourself, and identify grammar mistakes. The speaking practice tools in the Ovidhan app can help.
What is the quickest way to learn grammar?
The quickest way is a structured approach: follow a clear roadmap, practice daily, use interactive tools, take quizzes, and learn from your mistakes. Focus on high-priority topics first (Subject-Verb Agreement, Tenses, Articles, Prepositions).
