BCS Vocabulary – 100 Most Important Words for BCS Preliminary Exam

📅 July 1, 2026 · ⏱️ 10 min read · 📚 BCS Preparation

The English section of the BCS Preliminary exam is a decisive factor for thousands of aspirants every year. Approximately 32% of the English test items assess vocabulary knowledge, language usage, and idiomatic expressions[reference:0]. This means that a strong vocabulary is not optional – it is essential for success.

In this comprehensive guide, we have compiled 100 of the most frequently tested English words from previous BCS exams. Each word includes Bangla meaning, synonyms, antonyms, and example sentences to help you master them quickly.

📌 Did you know? The BCS Preliminary exam consists of 200 MCQ questions to be completed in 2 hours. The English section covers vocabulary, grammar, comprehension, and literature[reference:1].

Why Vocabulary is Critical for BCS

  • High weightage: Approximately one-third of the English questions test vocabulary directly[reference:2].
  • Synonyms and Antonyms: These are the most common question types in BCS English[reference:3].
  • Reading Comprehension: A strong vocabulary helps you understand passages faster and answer more accurately.
  • Idioms and Phrases: BCS frequently tests idioms, phrasal verbs, and one‑word substitutions[reference:4].

How to Use This Vocabulary List

  1. Learn 10 words daily – consistency beats cramming.
  2. Use flashcards – spaced repetition helps retain words long‑term.
  3. Practice with mock tests – test your knowledge regularly.
  4. Review weak areas – focus on words you find difficult.

BCS English Preparation Strategy

1. Daily Vocabulary Practice

Dedicate 15–20 minutes daily to learning new words. Use flashcards and spaced repetition for long-term retention. Apps like Ovidhan offer smart flashcards with SM-2 algorithm to help you remember words effectively.

2. Read Editorials Daily

Reading editorials of English national dailies enhances both vocabulary and the ability to express ideas effectively[reference:6]. Focus on opinion pieces, as they use advanced vocabulary in context.

3. Practice Grammar and Vocabulary Together

Revise one grammar topic each day while simultaneously learning new vocabulary words[reference:7]. This integrated approach improves overall English proficiency.

4. Take Mock Tests

Practice with 10,000+ MCQ questions to simulate exam conditions[reference:8]. Regular testing helps identify weak areas and builds confidence.

Common BCS Vocabulary Question Types

  • Synonyms: Find the closest meaning of a given word[reference:9].
  • Antonyms: Identify the opposite meaning[reference:10].
  • One‑word Substitutions: Replace a phrase with a single word[reference:11].
  • Idioms and Phrases: Understand the meaning of common expressions[reference:12].
  • Sentence Completion: Fill in blanks with appropriate vocabulary.
