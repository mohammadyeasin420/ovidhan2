1. 📌 Introduction

Every year, over 400,000 candidates apply for the BCS Preliminary exam in Bangladesh. However, only a small percentage – less than 1% – successfully qualify for the written exam. Among the various sections, English vocabulary consistently acts as a differentiator between successful and unsuccessful candidates.

Why does vocabulary matter so much? Because the BCS Preliminary English section tests vocabulary through synonyms, antonyms, idioms, phrases, and one-word substitutions. According to an analysis of the last 10 BCS exams (2016-2026), 30-40% of English questions test vocabulary directly.

📌 Did You Know? The BCS Preliminary exam consists of 200 MCQ questions to be completed in 2 hours. The English section typically carries 35 marks, with vocabulary-related questions making up 10-12 marks.

In this comprehensive guide, we have compiled 100 of the most frequently tested English words from previous BCS exams. Each word includes Bangla meaning, pronunciation (with phonetic guide), part of speech, difficulty level, synonyms, antonyms, example sentences, and memory tricks to help you retain each word effectively.

📅 This guide is reviewed and updated regularly. Last update: July 8, 2026

2. 🎯 Why Vocabulary Matters for BCS

  • High Weightage: Approximately 10-12 questions (30-35% of the English section) test vocabulary directly.
  • Quick Marks: Vocabulary questions can be answered quickly if you know the word, saving time for harder sections.
  • Repeated Words: Many vocabulary words appear again and again. Our list is based on analysis of the last 10 years.
  • Foundation for Other Sections: Strong vocabulary helps in reading comprehension, sentence correction, and grammar.
  • Competitive Edge: Since vocabulary is often neglected, mastering it gives you a significant advantage.
💡 BCS Tip: Many questions come directly from previous years. Practice with past papers to identify recurring patterns.

4. 📖 How to Study Vocabulary Effectively

  • Learn in Context: Don't just memorize meanings – read example sentences to understand usage.
  • Use Spaced Repetition: Review words at increasing intervals. The Ovidhan app uses the SM-2 algorithm for this.
  • Group by Difficulty: Start with easy words, then move to medium, hard, and elite levels.
  • Practice MCQs: Reading is not enough. Practice with multiple-choice questions to get exam-ready.
  • Teach Someone: Explaining a word to someone else reinforces your memory.

5. 📈 Previous Year BCS Vocabulary Analysis (2016-2026)

Based on a detailed analysis of the last 10 BCS Preliminary exams, here are the key trends:

  • Most Tested Topics: Synonyms and Antonyms are the most frequently tested, appearing in almost every exam.
  • Word Origins: Around 40% of words are derived from Latin, 30% from French, and 20% from Germanic roots.
  • Difficulty Trend: Over the last 5 years, the difficulty level of vocabulary has gradually increased.
  • Repetition Rate: Approximately 30% of vocabulary words appear in multiple exams.
  • Common Themes: Words related to behavior (e.g., Tenacious, Gregarious), emotions (e.g., Apathy, Zeal), and intellect (e.g., Eloquent, Meticulous) appear most frequently.
📊 BCS Insight: The word "Ubiquitous" has appeared in 7 out of the last 10 BCS exams. Words like "Meticulous" and "Tenacious" have appeared 5 times each. Our "Most Repeated Words" section highlights these patterns.

8. ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ignoring Context: Memorizing words without examples – always read the example sentence.
  • Neglecting Antonyms: BCS tests both synonyms and antonyms – learn both!
  • Skipping Review: New words without review = quick forgetting. Use spaced repetition.
  • Not Practicing MCQs: Reading is not enough – practice multiple-choice questions.
  • Relying Only on Bengali: Understand the English definition and usage too.
  • Learning in Isolation: Learn words in groups (e.g., synonyms together) for better retention.
